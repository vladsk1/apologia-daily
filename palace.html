The science
Why a Memory Palace works

The method of loci — the “memory palace” — is over 2,000 years old. Ancient orators used it to recall hour-long speeches, and every modern World Memory Champion still relies on it today.

Here’s the trick: your brain is poor at remembering abstract lists but exceptional at places and vivid images — you can still walk through your childhood home room by room. This tool hijacks that strength: each argument becomes a striking scene in a building you walk, so recalling it is as easy as remembering a room.

Two principles make it stick: spatial order (walking the rooms in sequence lets you recall the whole case in order) and retrieval practice (the Recall walk hides the meaning so you test yourself — recalling a fact strengthens memory far more than re-reading it).

Tip: the more exaggerated and vivid you picture each scene, the better it sticks. Strange beats sensible.
