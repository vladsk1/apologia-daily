Is it okay to have doubts as a Christian?

Yes - and you're in good company. The Bible is full of honest doubt: the psalmists cry "How long, O Lord?", John the Baptist sends to ask if Jesus is really the one, and Thomas refuses to believe until he sees. Jesus doesn't crush any of them; he meets them.

Doubt isn't the opposite of faith - apathy is. Often doubt is faith seeking understanding. The healthy response isn't to suppress the questions or to abandon ship, but to bring them into the light and investigate. That's the whole reason a place like this exists: there are real, satisfying answers to most of the hard questions, and a faith that has examined itself stands far stronger than one that never asked.
