Is there archaeological evidence for the Bible?

Yes - repeatedly. Archaeology can't prove theological claims, but it can test whether the Bible gets its world right, and again and again it does. The Pilate Stone confirms the Roman governor who tried Jesus. An ossuary names the high priest Caiaphas. Hezekiah's tunnel and the Tel Dan inscription ("House of David") confirm Old Testament figures sceptics once doubted.

Figures like Pontius Pilate, the politarchs of Thessalonica, and many cities and customs in Luke-Acts have been verified by inscriptions and excavation. A document that proves accurate on the checkable details earns trust on the rest. The biblical writers were rooted in real history.
