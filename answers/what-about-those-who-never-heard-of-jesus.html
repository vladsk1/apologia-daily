What about those who have never heard of Jesus?

This is one of the most morally serious questions anyone can ask, and it deserves to be honoured, not rushed. It seems deeply unfair: billions of people have lived and died without ever hearing of Jesus, through no fault of their own. If he really is the only way to God, are they simply condemned by the accident of when and where they were born?

Hold together two things Scripture holds firmly. Jesus really is the only way - 'no one comes to the Father except through me' (John 14:6) - and salvation, for anyone who has it, comes through his work alone. And God is perfectly just. Abraham's question, 'Shall not the Judge of all the earth do what is right?' (Genesis 18:25), is answered yes. Paul says those who never received God's written law will be judged not by information that never reached them, but by the light they did haveOn being judged by the light you haveIs sayingGod judges every person justly, by the truth actually available to them, and no one is condemned merely for never hearing a message that never reached them. He applies Christ's saving work as he alone knows how.Not sayingNot that anyone earns salvation by sincerity or a clear conscience apart from Christ. The light we are given leaves us needing grace — salvation is through Christ's work alone. - conscience, and the witness of creation itself (Romans 2:12-16; cf. 1:19-20). No one will be condemned for failing to believe a message they never heard.

What Scripture does not do is spell out exactly how God applies Christ's work to such a person, and sincere Christians hold a range of views on it. So here we should be genuinely humble rather than dogmatic in either direction. What we can say is this: the Judge is both perfectly just and the God who 'wants all people to be saved' (1 Timothy 2:4) - and he went to a cross to reach them. You can trust the fairness of the Judge even where you can't yet see the verdict. And if that fairness matters to you, it's worth remembering that you have heard, and can respond.
