Did Jesus actually claim to be God?

✓Fact-checked & reviewed for Nicene orthodoxyHow we review →

Yes - in both word and deed, and the strongest evidence is not the sayings a sceptic can dismiss as 'late.' Take our earliest Gospel, Mark, and watch his enemies react. When Jesus forgives a paralysed man's sins, the scribes are scandalised - "Who can forgive sins but God alone?" (Mark 2:7) - and they have the theology right; Jesus claims the divine prerogative anyway, then heals the man to vindicate the invisible claim. At his trial, asked if he is the Christ, he applies to himself the coming-on-the-clouds of Daniel 7 and the right hand of Psalm 110, and the high priest tears his robes and cries blasphemy (Mark 14:61-64). In both scenes it is his hostile opponents who confirm he claimed something only God could - the kind of detail the later church was unlikely to invent.

John says it more plainly - "Before Abraham was born, I AM" (John 8:58), taking God's own name from Exodus; "I and the Father are one" (John 10:30); and when Thomas cries "My Lord and my God!" Jesus accepts it rather than correcting him (John 20:28). Sceptics argue those loftiest sayings reflect John's own theology, but they only sharpen what is already in the earliest layers. The claim does not hang on a single verse - across the Gospels Jesus forgives sins, accepts worship, and makes himself the criterion of final judgement. It runs through the whole witness.
