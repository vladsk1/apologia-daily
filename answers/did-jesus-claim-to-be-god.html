Did Jesus actually claim to be God?

"Jesus never claimed to be God" is a popular line, but the New Testament tells a different story - in both words and actions. He said, "Before Abraham was born, I AM" (John 8:58), taking God's own name from Exodus; his hearers picked up stones, knowing exactly what he meant. He forgave sins - something only God can do - and accepted worship rather than refusing it.

He said, "I and the Father are one," and "Anyone who has seen me has seen the Father." When Thomas cried, "My Lord and my God!" Jesus didn't correct him. These are not the words of a mere teacher; they are a claim to be God in the flesh (John 1:1, 14).
