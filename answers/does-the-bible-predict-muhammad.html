Does the Bible predict Muhammad?

✓Fact-checked & reviewed for Nicene orthodoxyHow we review →

No - read in their own context, the passages Islam appeals to point to someone other than Muhammad. Islam teaches that Muhammad was foretold in the Bible, most often pointing to the 'Paraclete' Jesus promised in John 14-16 and the 'prophet like Moses' of Deuteronomy 18:15-18 - and neither fits. Jesus identifies the Paraclete explicitly as the Holy Spirit who would come upon the disciples and dwell in them (John 14:17, 26) — not a human prophet arriving centuries later in Arabia. The 'prophet like Moses' is drawn from among the Israelites ('from among your brothers'), and the New Testament applies it to Jesus (Acts 3:22).

The claim usually depends on substituting a similar-sounding Greek word (periklytos, 'praised one') for parakletos ('helper'), but no Greek manuscript supports that reading. None of this is meant to disparage Muslims' sincerity — it is simply that the texts, read honestly in context, do not point to Muhammad.
