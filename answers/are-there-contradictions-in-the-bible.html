Aren't there contradictions in the Bible?

Most alleged contradictions turn out to be differences of perspective, emphasis, or detail - the kind you'd expect from independent witnesses describing the same event. One Gospel mentions one angel at the tomb, another mentions two; that's not a contradiction (two includes one), it's two writers highlighting different things.

Ironically, if the accounts matched word-for-word, sceptics would (rightly) suspect collusion. The variations actually point to genuine, independent testimony. Many puzzles dissolve with cultural and historical context. There are hard passages worth wrestling with honestly - but "the Bible is riddled with contradictions" doesn't survive a careful, case-by-case look.
