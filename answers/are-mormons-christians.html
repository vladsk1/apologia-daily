Are Mormons Christians?

This is one of those questions where the honest answer is: it depends what you mean, and the distinction matters more than a yes or no. If "Christian" means someone who reveres Jesus Christ, seeks to follow him, and centers their life on him, then Latter-day Saints plainly qualify - many are devout, Christ-focused people, and it would be ungracious to pretend otherwise. That sincerity deserves respect, not a sneer.

But if "Christian" means holding the historic doctrine of God confessed in the Nicene Creed - the creed shared by Catholics, Orthodox, and Protestants alike - then there is a real, and openly acknowledged, difference. Latter-day Saints themselves distinguish their teaching from the creeds. Their official Gospel Topics teaching states that the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are "three separate beings united in purpose," with the Father and Son having "tangible bodies of flesh and bones." Historic Christianity confesses one God, numerically one, in three coequal and coeternal persons, who did not become God and before whom there was no God. Those two pictures of God cannot both be right.

So the fair thing is to judge the doctrine, not the hearts. On the historic definition of the term, the LDS doctrine of God differs from Christian orthodoxy - and this is a difference the Church itself affirms. That is a genuine theological disagreement worth exploring with respect, not a verdict on anyone's sincerity or eternal standing, which is God's to render, not ours.
