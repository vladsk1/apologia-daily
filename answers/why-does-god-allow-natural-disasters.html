Why does God allow natural disasters?

This is the hardest form of the problem, and it should be said plainly. A tsunami or an earthquake needs no villain - no free human chose it - yet it drowns thousands, infants included. You can trace moral evil back to human freedom; but here it's nature itself killing indiscriminately. A believer who waves that away hasn't understood the question.

So let's be honest: this is exactly where the usual 'free will' response runs thin, and pretending otherwise helps no one. Two things can be said, and neither is a complete answer. First, a stable, law-governed physical world is the precondition for embodied life and real choices at all - and the same plate tectonics that trigger earthquakes also recycle carbon and build the very land we live on. You can't obviously have one without the other in a lawful universe. Second, Jesus flatly refused to read disaster as a scorecard of guilt: told of people killed when a tower collapsed, he denied they were worse sinners than anyone else (Luke 13:4-5). Victims are not being singled out for punishment.

Christianity also refuses to call the present world the way it's meant to stay. Scripture says creation itself is 'groaning,' waiting to be set right (Romans 8:22). And notice something: to call a tsunami a tragedy - rather than a meaningless rearrangement of atoms - is already to assume the world ought to be otherwise. That moral intuition sits far more easily with a good Creator than with blind matter. The mystery here stays real. But so does the hope.
