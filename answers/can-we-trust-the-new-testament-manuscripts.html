Can we trust the New Testament manuscripts?

By any standard historians apply to ancient writings, yes. Compare the numbers: Caesar's Gallic Wars survives in about 10 copies, the average classical work in a few dozen to a few hundred. The New Testament survives in more than 5,800 Greek manuscripts, some within a generation or two of the originals.

No one doubts what Caesar or Tacitus wrote, despite far thinner evidence. The New Testament gives us more manuscripts, earlier, with more agreement. The question was never whether the text was copied - of course it was - but whether it was copied reliably. By the historian's own measuring stick, the answer is yes.
