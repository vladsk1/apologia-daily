Wasn't Jesus just a good moral teacher?

It sounds respectful, but it's the one option Jesus didn't leave open. A good moral teacher doesn't claim to forgive sins, accept worship, and share God's own name - unless those claims are true.

C.S. Lewis put it bluntly: a man who said the sorts of things Jesus said would either be a liar, a lunatic, or exactly who he claimed to be. He could be the Lord, or a deceiver, or self-deluded - but not merely a wise human teacher, because wise human teachers don't make themselves the center of their own message. You're free to reject his claim, but "great moral teacher" isn't a way to dodge it.
