How can a loving God send people to hell?

It helps to reframe the picture. God doesn't gleefully dispatch people to hell; the New Testament shows a God who "wants all people to be saved" (1 Timothy 2:4) and who went to the cross to rescue them.

But genuine love requires the freedom to refuse it - a love you cannot say no to wouldn't be love at all. Hell, as writers like C.S. Lewis describe it, is what it means for God to finally honor the choice of someone who has spent their whole life saying, "Leave me alone." The door, Lewis said, is locked from the inside. God's justice and his respect for human freedom meet there - and the cross shows how far he goes to spare us it.
