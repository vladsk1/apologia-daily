Do Muslims and Christians worship the same God?

This is a question people feel strongly about - and one on which faithful, orthodox Christians genuinely disagree - so it deserves precision rather than a quick yes or no. There is a real overlap: both are monotheists who trace their worship back to the God of Abraham, the maker of heaven and earth, and both are fiercely committed to his oneness. A Muslim who recoils from the Trinity is guarding God's oneness, tawhid, and dreads the sin of shirk, associating a partner with God - an instinct against idolatry Christians can respect, since they too worship one God and reject every rival deity. Where the two genuinely differ is in what they believe that one God is like in his own inner life. Christians do not believe in three gods; the church has condemned that as heresy for nearly two thousand years. Nor, despite one Qur'anic passage naming God, Jesus, and Mary, has any Christian creed ever placed Mary in the Godhead, and Christians reject that idea as firmly as any Muslim would. The Trinity is not a partner added to God from outside; that would indeed be shirk. It is a claim about what the one God eternally is within himself: an eternal communion of Father, Son, and Spirit in a single, undivided essence. Even the word son is meant relationally, not biologically, Arabic Christians say ibn Allah, never walad Allah, which would imply a physical child that Christians deny as strongly as the Qur'an does.

So the most honest answer is neither a confident 'yes, the same God' nor a dismissive 'no, a different God entirely.' There is genuine overlap in the One both intend - the Creator, the God of Abraham - but the God Christians actually worship is triune, and a picture of God that rejects the Son does not reach him as he has made himself known. That is not a difference to smooth over but to explore honestly and gently, as a conversation between people who both take God's oneness with utter seriousness - and it is why Christians long for their Muslim neighbours to come to know the Father through the Son.
