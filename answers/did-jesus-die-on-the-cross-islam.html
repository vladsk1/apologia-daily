Did Jesus die on the cross, or does the Quran deny it?

Here the two faiths genuinely diverge, and the Muslim position deserves to be stated fairly rather than brushed aside. The key text is Surah 4:157, which on its dominant reading denies that Jesus was crucified - "they did not kill him, nor did they crucify him, but it was made to appear so to them." The classical Islamic tradition often reads this as a substitution: God rescued his prophet and another was crucified in his place. It is worth adding that some Muslim scholars read the verse more subtly - as denying that Jesus' enemies truly triumphed over him, rather than denying his death outright - and a Christian who insists the Quran must mean the crude substitution story is not reading it as carefully as its best interpreters do.

That said, on the historical question the evidence is unusually one-sided. Jesus' death by crucifixion under Pontius Pilate is regarded by historians across the spectrum as among the most certain facts we have about him. The agnostic scholar Bart Ehrman calls it as sure as anything historical can be, and it is attested not only in the Gospels but in non-Christian sources like the Roman historian Tacitus. Crucifixion was also the last thing early followers would invent about a Messiah - a crucified Messiah was a scandal, not a selling point.

So the tension is real and should be named without triumphalism. The Quran, on its usual reading, sets aside an event that the historical record attests as firmly as almost anything from antiquity. That does not settle every question of faith, but on this particular point the historian and the Gospel writers stand together: Jesus really did die on a Roman cross.
