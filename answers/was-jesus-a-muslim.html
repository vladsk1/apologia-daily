Was Jesus a Muslim?

Muslims often say Jesus (Isa) was a Muslim, meaning a 'submitter' to the one God — and in that broad sense, yes, Jesus submitted to his Father perfectly. But 'submitter to God' is not the same as a follower of the religion of Islam, which is defined by the testimony that Muhammad is God's messenger and by the Quran — both from the seventh century, six hundred years after Jesus. Jesus was a first-century Jew who never heard of Muhammad and kept the Torah, not Islamic law.

More decisively, the Jesus of the historical record did things no Muslim ever could: he forgave sins in his own name (Mark 2:5-7), accepted worship (John 20:28-29), and claimed to share the Father's glory (John 17:5) — which on Islam's own terms would be shirk, impossible for a prophet. So either we trust the Gospels, and Jesus was no Muslim, or we discard them, and there is no historical 'Muslim Jesus' left at all. His submission to the Father was real — the submission of God the Son, who became human to save us (Philippians 2:6-8).
