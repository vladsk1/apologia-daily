Were the Gospels written by eyewitnesses?

The Gospels were written within the lifetime of people who knew Jesus - decades after the events, not centuries. They draw directly on eyewitness testimony: Matthew and John are tied to apostles, Mark to Peter's preaching, and Luke explicitly says he investigated "those who from the first were eyewitnesses" (Luke 1:2).

They also carry the marks of real memory - accurate, incidental details of first-century Palestinian geography, names, customs, and politics that a later, distant author would be unlikely to get right. They read like reports anchored in testimony, not like myths composed long afterward in a faraway place.
