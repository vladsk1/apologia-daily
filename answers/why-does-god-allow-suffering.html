If God is good, why is there so much suffering?

This is the deepest and most painful objection, and it deserves honesty, not a tidy slogan. Innocent people suffer; prayers seem unanswered. That weight is real.

But notice something. The moment you call suffering not just unpleasant but genuinely evil - truly wrong - you've assumed a real standard of how things ought to be. A purely indifferent universe has no "ought"; it can host pain, but not evil. So the very force of the objection borrows from a moral order that fits God better than a godless world. Christianity doesn't offer a formula; it offers a God who entered the suffering himself, and who promises to "wipe every tear from their eyes" (Revelation 21:4).
