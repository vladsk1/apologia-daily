Has the Bible been changed over the centuries?

It's a common worry - a "game of telephone" over two thousand years. But that's not how manuscript copying works, and the evidence shows the opposite. The New Testament survives in over 5,800 Greek manuscripts, plus many thousands more in other languages - by far the best-attested text of the ancient world.

That abundance is a strength: scholars can compare copies and map exactly where differences crept in. The vast majority are spelling and word-order variations, and not one core Christian doctrine hangs on a disputed line. The Dead Sea Scrolls confirmed the same for the Old Testament. The text wasn't changed; it was carefully preserved.
