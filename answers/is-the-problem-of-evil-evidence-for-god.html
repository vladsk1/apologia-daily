Could evil actually be evidence FOR God?

It sounds backwards, but follow the logic. To say something is objectively evil - not just "I dislike it" - you have to measure it against a real standard of good. Where does that standard come from?

In a purely material universe of atoms and forces, there is no way things "ought" to be; there are only events. So the atheist who presses the problem of evil is quietly standing on a moral reality that atheism can't easily supply. As C.S. Lewis recounted, his own argument against God from the world's injustice forced the question: where did he get the idea of "just" and "unjust"? Evil is a genuine weight on faith - but it's an even harder problem for a world without God.
