Where do morality and human rights come from without God?

Most of us know that some things - torturing a child for fun, for example - are not merely distasteful but genuinely wrong. The question is what could make them really wrong.

If there is no God, "evil" collapses into "something I happen to dislike," and cruelty becomes a matter of taste. But we don't actually believe that; we believe such acts would be wrong even if everyone approved of them. A moral law that binds everyone, everywhere, points beyond human preference to a moral lawgiver. Your deepest moral instinct is pointing at something - and Someone - real. "He has shown you... what is good" (Micah 6:8).
