Are Jehovah's Witnesses Christians?

As with similar questions, the honest answer turns on what "Christian" means, and the distinction is worth making carefully rather than crudely. If it means someone who honors Jesus, studies the Bible diligently, and tries to obey God, then Jehovah's Witnesses plainly qualify. The person at your door is, in most cases, sincere, often strikingly well-versed in Scripture, and genuinely trying to honor the oneness of God. That is not nothing, and it deserves respect.

But if "Christian" means holding the faith confessed in the Nicene Creed - the creed shared by Catholics, Orthodox, and Protestants - then there is a real difference, and Witnesses are the first to say so. The Watchtower teaches that only the Father is God in the full sense; the Son is God's first and greatest creation, and the Holy Spirit is an impersonal "active force," not a person. Historic Christianity confesses that the Son is of the same nature as the Father - coequal, coeternal - one God in three persons. The question is precise: is Jesus a creature or the Creator? The historic church answers Creator, and points to texts like John 20:28, where Thomas calls the risen Jesus "My Lord and my God" and is blessed rather than corrected.

So the fair statement is that Witnesses self-identify as Christian and love Jesus - grant that warmly - but by the historic creedal definition their doctrine of God differs at its center. That is a disagreement about who Jesus is, to be pursued gently, one text at a time - not a judgment on anyone's heart, which belongs to God alone.
