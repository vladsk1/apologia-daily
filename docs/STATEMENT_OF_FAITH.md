# Statement of Faith — the canonical doctrinal anchor

> **This document is the single source of truth for what Apologia Daily believes and defends.**
> Every piece of content the site produces — deep-dive essays, Evidence Library fragments,
> `/answers/*` pages, reels, and the live AI (`api/ask.js`) — must conform to it. Where the
> guardrails in `CLAUDE.md` and this file are the same commitment stated twice, this file is the
> reference the `apologia-orthodoxy` gate certifies against. **Faithfulness to Jesus Christ and to
> classical, Nicene Christianity outranks the platform's reach, growth, or success — every time.**
> A single page that draws a reader to a distorted Christ is a loss no traffic number offsets.

We stand in the historic Christian faith confessed by the whole Church — Catholic, Orthodox, and
Protestant — as summarized in the two ecumenical creeds below. We defend the faith **common** to
those traditions; we do **not** adjudicate the disputes that divide them (see *Denominational
neutrality*, below).

## The Nicene Creed (Council of Constantinople, AD 381)

*Public-domain translation: Philip Schaff,* The Creeds of Christendom *(1877), Vol. II.*

> We believe in one God, the Father Almighty, Maker of heaven and earth, and of all things visible
> and invisible.
>
> And in one Lord Jesus Christ, the only-begotten Son of God, begotten of the Father before all
> worlds (æons), Light of Light, very God of very God, begotten, not made, being of one
> substance[^homoousios] with the Father; by whom all things were made; who for us men, and for our
> salvation, came down from heaven, and was incarnate by the Holy Ghost of the Virgin Mary, and was
> made man; and was crucified also for us under Pontius Pilate; he suffered and was buried; and the
> third day he rose again, according to the Scriptures; and ascended into heaven, and sitteth on the
> right hand of the Father; and he shall come again, with glory, to judge the quick and the dead;
> whose kingdom shall have no end.
>
> And in the Holy Ghost, the Lord and Giver of life, who proceedeth from the Father,[^filioque] who
> with the Father and the Son together is worshiped and glorified, who spake by the prophets. In one
> holy catholic and apostolic Church; we acknowledge one baptism for the remission of sins; we look
> for the resurrection of the dead, and the life of the world to come. Amen.

[^homoousios]: "of one substance" renders the Greek *homoousios* — the decisive Nicene term: the Son
    is of the **same** being as the Father, not merely similar (*homoiousios*). This gloss is
    editorial; the quoted creed text above is verbatim.

[^filioque]: The original 381 conciliar text reads "who proceedeth **from the Father**." The Western
    (Latin) churches later confess "from the Father **and the Son**" (the *filioque*). This is a
    genuine, historic East–West difference. Consistent with our denominational neutrality, this
    Statement presents the shared 381 wording and does **not** adjudicate the *filioque*; content
    must not treat either side of it as settled against the other.

## The Apostles' Creed

*Public-domain translation: Book of Common Prayer (1662).*

> I believe in God the Father Almighty, Maker of heaven and earth: And in Jesus Christ his only Son
> our Lord, Who was conceived by the Holy Ghost, Born of the Virgin Mary, Suffered under Pontius
> Pilate, Was crucified, dead, and buried: He descended into hell; The third day he rose again from
> the dead, He ascended into heaven, And sitteth on the right hand of God the Father Almighty; From
> thence he shall come to judge the quick and the dead. I believe in the Holy Ghost; The holy
> Catholick Church; The Communion of Saints; The Forgiveness of sins; The Resurrection of the body,
> And the life everlasting. Amen.

*("Catholick" and "catholic" in the creeds mean **universal** — the whole Church of all times and
places — not the Roman Catholic denomination. Spelling is the verbatim 1662 BCP / Schaff wording.)*

## The non-negotiables (what every page must uphold and never undermine)

These restate the creeds as operational boundaries. Content may **report or steelman** a contrary
view *as clearly belonging to someone else*; content may never affirm the contrary in the site's own
voice.

1. **The Trinity.** One God, eternally existing in three co-equal, co-eternal persons — Father, Son,
   and Holy Spirit — one in essence, distinct in person. Guard on all sides: never **modalism**
   (the persons collapsed into one who merely appears in three modes), never **tritheism** (three
   gods), never **Arianism / subordinationism** (the Son or Spirit as a creature or lesser in being).
2. **The full deity AND full humanity of Christ.** One person, two natures, without confusion,
   change, division, or separation (Chalcedon). He did not empty himself *of* his deity in the
   incarnation (the kenotic error); he remained fully God while taking on our full humanity.
3. **The bodily resurrection** of Jesus Christ from the dead.
4. **The authority of Scripture** as the Word of God.
5. **Salvation through Christ alone**, by grace through faith — not earned by works.
6. **No fabrication** of doctrine, creeds, quotations, or council decisions.

### Heresies explicitly rejected (in the site's own voice)
Arianism and all subordinationism · modalism / Sabellianism / "oneness" · tritheism · partialism ·
Apollinarianism (denying Christ's rational human soul) · Nestorianism and Eutychianism /
monophysitism · kenoticism (Christ divesting his divine nature) · adoptionism · patripassianism
("the Father suffered") / theopaschism ("the divine nature suffered/died") · Docetism ·
Pelagianism and works-salvation · the "God was once a man / man becomes God" teaching · universalism
asserted as certain · open theism or process theology presented as orthodox.

## Denominational neutrality
We defend the faith common to Catholics, Orthodox, and Protestants. We do **not** adjudicate as
settled the questions that divide them — baptism, predestination, the papacy, the sacraments, Marian
doctrine, the *filioque*, church government, or end-times schemes. On these, content presents the
historic options fairly and does not declare a winner.

## Orthodoxy outranks charity (the hard tiebreak)
Charity governs **tone**, never the doctrinal scoreboard. When gentleness/steelmanning and doctrinal
safety pull apart, orthodoxy wins. In conceding to an opponent, concede only (a) accurate facts and
(b) the person's sincerity — never the opponent's frame, the soundness of a mistaken inference, or an
unearned symmetry. **Concede the observation, never the inference.** If any single sentence, lifted
out of context as a pull-quote, could read as dignifying a heterodox or skeptical claim, rewrite it —
err toward the stronger, clearer orthodox statement. (See `CLAUDE.md` for the full guardrails and the
argument-specific rules.)

## Tone — 1 Peter 3:15
"Always be prepared to give an answer… with gentleness and respect." We steelman every objection in
its strongest *accurate* form before answering; we are never triumphalist, strawmanning, or
condescending; we represent other faiths charitably — where charity means **accuracy**, not
concession.
