## Thesis (our words)
A careful, orthodox, devotional walkthrough of the entire Creed, arguing that Nicaea did **not invent** a new
doctrine but **clarified and re-articulated the apostolic faith** against Arianism ("there was a *when* he
was not"). The technical vocabulary (*homoousios*, begotten, procession, spiration) is a **pastoral
necessity** — clarifying biblical-but-ambiguous language ("God," "Lord," "Son of God") against a heresy that
could otherwise borrow the same words. Structure: the Trinity is the *skeleton*, the Incarnation the *heart*.

## Argument map (the 20 "chapters" → primaries/terms to VERIFY)
- **History & framing:** Nicaea I (325, ~318 bishops, convened by Constantine, vs. **Arius**) → expanded at
  **Constantinople I (381)** = the "Niceno-Constantinopolitan" creed (325 had ~318 bishops — the *traditional/symbolic* figure, echoing Gen 14:14; actual attendance ~250–320) (biggest change: the Holy Spirit
  article). The Creed is *biblical in substance*, technical in form; "good philosophy must exist because bad
  philosophy needs to be answered" (C. S. Lewis).
- **The Father Almighty / one God:** *pantokratōr* = "ruler of all" (Rev.); monotheism as a persecuted
  battlefront — **Justin Martyr**: Christians called "atheists" as to pagan gods but not "the most true God";
  the "appropriation" doctrine (an attribute ascribed to one person, not exclusive to him — **Aquinas**,
  *ST* I q38 a7); *why not Unitarian?* — "and in one Lord Jesus Christ… *homoousios*" answers it.
- **The Son — the core:** *one Lord Jesus Christ* (kyrios; 1 Cor 8:6; Phil 2 → Isaiah 45, N. T. Wright);
  *only-begotten* (*monogenēs* = "only born," not made-in-time; Jude 25 "before all the ages"); **God from
  God, light from light, true God from true God** (the "X-from-X" formula; Heb 1:3 radiance; Turretin; John Behr on "true God" excluding a lesser/intermediary deity); **begotten, not made** (the Creator/creation
  line — no intermediary); **homoousios / "consubstantial"** = *the* key word, "same essence" (vs.
  **homoiousios**, "similar" — the famous single-**iota** shorthand; *precisely*: homoiousios was the mid-4th-c.
  **semi-Arian (Homoiousian)** banner that arose AFTER 325, not Arius's own word (strict Arians said
  *anomoios/heteroousios*), and the "battle over an iota" is a vivid simplification). *Baggage:* the term
  was condemned at **Antioch 268** (Paul of Samosata context) and used earlier by Gnostics — so the Arians
  attacked it as unbiblical; **Athanasius** (*On the Council of Nicaea* / *De Decretis*, ~350s) led the
  defense: image/radiance implies *same* nature, not merely like.
- **The Incarnation (10 verbs):** "came down from heaven" = **metaphor, not geography** (Augustine: "he came
  to a place where he was already"; **Gregory of Nazianzus**: "he remained what he was and took up what he
  was not") → **anti-kenotic**: the Son did *not* relinquish deity (**ties directly to
  `answers/did-jesus-empty-himself-of-his-deity.html`**); "incarnate by the Holy Spirit and the Virgin Mary"
  (Luke 1); "crucified under Pontius Pilate" = a *public historical anchor* (Acts speeches; 1 Tim 6:13);
  "suffered, buried" — the burial as full solidarity "womb to tomb" (**Gregory of Nyssa**); rose (1 Cor 15
  firstfruits); ascended/seated (Ps 110; Torrance — ascension not geographical either); comes again in glory;
  "his kingdom shall have no end" (added vs. 325, against **Marcellus of Ancyra**, who read 1 Cor 15:24–28 as the Son's *reign/distinct existence* being temporary — a modalist-tending view).
- **The Holy Spirit (the 381 expansion):** vs. the **Pneumatomachi (traditionally called "Macedonians")** who denied the
  Spirit's deity; **Gregory of Nazianzus** prominent; **Basil of Caesarea**, *On the Holy Spirit*. "Lord and
  giver of life" (2 Cor 3; John 6:63); the Spirit as the mutual *love* of Father and Son (Augustine, Anselm);
  **procession / spiration** (John 15:26) — with Gregory of Nazianzus's caution against prying too far;
  "co-adored and co-glorified"; "spoke through the prophets" (inspiration).
- **The final articles:** one holy catholic apostolic church; one baptism for the forgiveness of sins (from
  Acts 2 — Ortlund notes historic **Baptists** affirm the Creed without baptismal regeneration); resurrection
  of the body + life of the world to come.

## ⚠ THE FILIOQUE — handle with strict denominational neutrality (our hard guardrail)
The single most important flag. Ortlund reaches the **filioque** ("and the Son") and, *as a Protestant*,
**affirms** it — but he models the honest history: (a) it was **not** in the original 381 creed; (b) it arose
later in **local usage in Spain (Toledo)** and spread west; (c) it is a **major East–West dividing** issue;
(d) following **Turretin**, it "should not have been the occasion for schism"; and (e) the "from
the Father *through* the Son" reading (defended in the *Letter to Marinus* **attributed to** Maximus the
Confessor — authenticity debated; the idea has Cappadocian roots) is a possible ecumenical bridge.
- **Our rule (non-negotiable): denominational neutrality.** We defend the faith common to Catholics,
  Orthodox, and Protestants and **do NOT adjudicate the filioque.** So any content drawing on this must
  present the filioque **neutrally — both traditions, no side taken** — never Ortlund's "I affirm it"
  position. Our `what-we-believe.html` already does this (the clause is **bracketed neutrally**). Keep it
  that way. This is exactly the kind of intra-Christian dispute the guardrails forbid us to settle.

## Guardrail fit
- **Deity/Trinity = highest-stakes.** Anything from this reaching an essay/answer/brief needs
  **dual-consensus** (apologia-orthodoxy AND apologia-neutrality), plus strict Nicene precision (no
  subordinationism/modalism drift; the eternal-generation/procession language is orthodox but easily
  misread — use `orthonote` clarifiers where a compressed reader could stumble).
- **Denominational neutrality** beyond the filioque too: baptismal regeneration, the "marks of the church,"
  Marian language — Ortlund flags these as *post-creed* Protestant/Catholic/Orthodox disagreements; present
  them neutrally, don't adjudicate.
- **Good tone to emulate** (rare in this batch): gracious, worshipful, ecumenical, honest about what the East
  gets right. A positive model, unlike the Islam-apologetics videos.
