## Thesis (our words)
The oral **creedal / confessional traditions embedded in the New Testament** — hymns, confessions,
and the "sermon summaries" in Acts — *predate the books that contain them*. They are the church's
teaching in the ~20-year window between the crucifixion (30 or 33 AD) and the first NT book
(1 Thessalonians, ~50 AD — the earliest *undisputed* letter; a minority date Galatians ~48), and the earliest of them may sit within ~5 years of the cross. Habermas's
angle here is not the resurrection (his usual topic) but **Christology**: these earliest layers already
carry a **fully divine** view of Jesus — the position now labelled **"Early High Christology"** (its
proponents nicknamed the "Early High Christology Club"), an **influential and much-debated** position in
current NT scholarship (contested by the gradual-development camp — Ehrman, McGrath, Casey). The move:
because this material is *early* (days-to-months,
from the Jerusalem apostles) and *pre-literary*, a high Christology cannot be dismissed as a late Hellenistic
overlay — it is in the bedrock.

## Argument map (beats → primaries to VERIFY)
1. **Creeds predate their books.** Paul explicitly hands on *received* tradition — "I delivered to you
   what I also received." → **Primaries:** 1 Cor 15:1–7 (the gospel/creed); 1 Cor 11:23–26 (the Lord's
   Supper tradition). Both are Paul quoting *prior* material.
2. **Dating chain.** NT books on critical (late) dating: Mark ~65–75, Matt/Luke ~80–85, John ~95; Paul's
   letters earlier, 1 Thess ~50; Paul/Peter/James martyred early-to-mid 60s. → **Primaries/leads to check:**
   the martyrdom dates (James via Josephus, ***Antiquities* 20.200** ≈ 62 AD); Paul's Jerusalem chronology in
   **Gal 1:18–19; 2:1–2** (conversion ~2–3 yrs post-cross; Jerusalem visits fix an early terminus).
3. **Where the creeds are.** Chiefly Paul's epistles + the kerygmatic summaries in **Acts 1–5; 10 (Peter);
   13, 17 (Paul).** Habermas: ~80% of the creedal passages concern **deity, death, resurrection** —
   *attribute the ~80% to him; it's a popular-level figure, not a datum we can independently verify.*
4. **Why trust oral tradition?** (the "telephone game" objection) — answered by earliness + apostolic
   origin + (Paul's own claim) inspired proclamation → **1 Cor 2:13.**
5. **The Christology itself — 5 of a possible ~8 points, deliberately chosen from passages even the most
   critical scholars grant** (Mark is "the critic's favourite gospel," so he starts there):
   - **(i) Jesus forgives sins → the divine prerogative.** Mark 2:1–12: "your sins are forgiven"; the
     scribes: "who can forgive sins but God alone?" → blasphemy charge. → **Primary: Mark 2:5–10.**
   - **(ii) The trial: Son of Man on the clouds.** Before the high priest, Jesus affirms he is the
     Messiah and applies **Daniel 7:13–14** to himself (coming on the clouds) + Ps 110 session → torn
     robes, "blasphemy." → **Primaries: Mark 14:61–64; Daniel 7:13–14; Ps 110:1.** (Habermas cites a
     scholar on the trial as blasphemy — **lead: Darrell Bock**, *Blasphemy and Exaltation in Judaism*.)
   - **(iii) Philippians 2 — an early hymn giving Jesus the divine name/worship.** *Widely* (not
     universally — Fee dissents on both the pre-Pauline and "hymn" claims) regarded as pre-Pauline;
     "every knee shall bow" quotes **Isaiah 45:23**, a text that in Isaiah is spoken by **Yahweh of
     himself** (45:21–23) — now applied to Jesus. ✓ identification verified. → **Primaries: Phil 2:6–11; Isa 45:23.**
   - **(iv) Romans 10 — an early confession + a Yahweh text applied to Jesus.** "If you confess with
     your mouth that Jesus is Lord…"; "everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved" quotes
     **Joel 2:32**, where "the LORD" is **Yahweh.** ✓ verified. → **Primaries: Rom 10:9, 10:13; Joel
     2:32 (cite as "Joel 2:32 (MT 3:5)" — the Hebrew versification differs, to preempt a "wrong verse" objection).**
   - **(v) Maranatha — an *Aramaic* prayer to "the Lord."** 1 Cor 16:22: "*Marana tha*" = "Our Lord,
     come." Aramaic → traces to the earliest Aramaic-speaking Jerusalem/Palestinian community, and the
     community *prays to Jesus as "Lord"* and asks him to come — a divine role. ✓ verified. → **Primary:
     1 Cor 16:22 (cf. Rev 22:20; Didache 10:6). Caveat to carry: the consonants also admit *maran atha*
     = "Our Lord has come" (perfect); the imperative eschatological reading is standard but not certain —
     acknowledge the ambiguity, don't assert one division.**
   - Also mentioned in passing: **Romans 9:5** (Christ "who is God over all"); **Colossians 1:15–20** (an
     early hymn); Matthew 28:17 (worship of the risen Jesus).

## Named-scholar LEADS (verified 2026-07-21 by apologia-citations — corrections folded in)
- **C. H. Dodd** ✓, *The Apostolic Preaching and Its Developments* (1936), Cambridge NT scholar — the Acts
  kerygma reflects what the original apostles preached.
- **Richard Bauckham** ✓ (living NT scholar; *Jesus and the God of Israel*) — the creeds are traditions
  received from the Jerusalem apostles. *(Drop the talk's "most prominent living" superlative.)*
- **Oscar Cullmann** ✓ — **French/Alsatian Lutheran** theologian (NOT "German," as the caption implied;
  born Strasbourg 1902); *The Earliest Christian Confessions* — the confessions are at least from apostolic times.
- **Howard Clark Kee** ✓ (mainstream/critical NT scholar) — traditions can be examined critically like
  courtroom evidence. **⚠ CORRECTION: Kee was NOT a Jesus-Seminar member — he was a public *critic* of it.**
  Do NOT tag him "Jesus Seminar" or count him as a Jesus-Seminar concession.

  the earliest days/months; a central EHCC figure.
- **James D. G. Dunn** ✓ — the creed formed "within months" of the crucifixion (*Jesus Remembered*, 854–55).
  *(Attribute "within months" to Dunn ONLY, never our own voice; our voice = "~2–5 years.")*
- **Marcus Borg** ✓ — **a prominent *fellow* of the Jesus Seminar (NOT a "co-founder"** — founded by Robert
  Funk, 1985, with Crossan); on historical grounds one can hardly deny Jesus was a healer. *(The healer line

- **Darrell Bock** ✓ — *Blasphemy and Exaltation in Judaism and the Final Examination of Jesus* (WUNT 2/106,

- **"Early High Christology Club"** ✓ — a real semi-jocular SBL self-designation (~1990s); associated with
  Hurtado, Bauckham, David Capes, Alan Segal, Michael Bird.

## Guardrail fit (calibration flags — read before pulling)
- **Popular-level overstatement to watch.** "Universally / unanimously called an early hymn" (Phil 2),
  "most influential view" (EHCC), and round martyrdom/composition dates are the kinds of claims to *soften
  to what the primary + scholarship actually support* — cite the datum, not the superlative. The site
  already does this well (our voice says the 1 Cor 15 creed is "within ~2–5 years"; "within months" is
  attributed to Dunn — keep it that way).
- **Deity content = dual-consensus.** Anything from this that reaches an essay/card/answer or a brief must
  clear `apologia-orthodoxy` AND `apologia-neutrality` (deity/Trinity set), not just one gate.
- **The manuscripts-≠-truth rule doesn't apply** (this is dating/authenticity of *tradition*, not textual
  transmission), but the resurrection rule does: **lead with the 1 Cor 15:3–7 creed**, never frame the
  evidence as "merely written decades later."
- **Neutrality / accuracy-as-charity.** The "critical scholars concede X" framing is genuinely strong but
  must be stated precisely (Kee/Borg are Jesus-Seminar figures conceding *earliness*/*a historical core*,
  not conceding deity) — don't inflate a concession of *early tradition* into a concession of *Christ's
  deity*.
