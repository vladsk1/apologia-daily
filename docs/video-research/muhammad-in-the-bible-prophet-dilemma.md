## Thesis (our words)
Because **Surah 7:157** says the "unlettered prophet" is "written with them in the Torah and the Gospel,"
Muslims must locate Muhammad in the Bible. The speaker's move — the **"Biblical Prophet Dilemma"** — is that
this can't succeed *either way*: **(Horn 1)** if the Bible predicts *no* future scripture-bearing prophet
after Jesus, then 7:157 is false → Islam false; **(Horn 2)** if it *does* predict a future prophet, that
prophet (on the Bible's own authority, which the Muslim is now invoking) must **confirm** the Bible's
theology — a crucified, risen, divine Christ — which Muhammad contradicts, so the predicted prophet can't be
Muhammad → Islam false. He pairs this with a **circularity charge** (Muslims use the Qur'an to pick the
"true" parts of the Bible, then use those parts to confirm the Qur'an) and the **Ahmadiyya parallel** (Muslims
reject Mirza Ghulam Ahmad for contradicting Sunni/Shia Islam — the very logic by which Christians reject
Muhammad). The rest is a proof-text-by-proof-text rebuttal.

## Argument map (beats → primaries to VERIFY — the topic is a certified answer already)
1. **The frame — the "Biblical Prophet Dilemma."** → 7:157; the general logic (a genuinely *net-new
   argumentative move* vs our answer page — see live-door note).
2. **Circularity + the Ahmadiyya parallel.** → **Mirza Ghulam Ahmad** (1835–1908, Ahmadiyya founder, claimed
   to be the Promised Messiah/Mahdi). **⚠ Fairness caveat: mainstream Sunni & Shia Islam reject Ahmadis as
   non-Muslim** (over the finality-of-prophethood), so the "same-logic" parallel is rhetorically neat but a
   Muslim will fairly object that Ahmad isn't a Muslim authority — flag it, don't present it as a clean
   gotcha. The dawah foil is **"Abraham Fulfilled"** (Sapience Institute, **2024**; Abu Zakariya, Adnan
   Rashid, Zakir Hussain — verified), the cumulative "Arabian prophecies" case (the video says it concedes the
   circular move at the end; *verify that quotation against the book*).
3. **Deuteronomy 18:15,18** ("a prophet like you *from among your brothers*"). → "brothers" here = **covenant
   kin — Israelites (Deut 1:16; 3:18; 24:7), at most Edom/Esau (Deut 2:4; 23:7), but NEVER Ishmaelites**
   (correct the video's "always Israelite" overstatement). **Strongest verse: Deut 17:15** — the king must be
   "from among your brothers… not a *foreigner*," setting brother *against* foreigner. The "from among you"
   qualifier (v. 15) is **pre-Islamic** — anchor on the **pre-Christian Septuagint** (Deut 18:15 LXX, *ek tōn
   adelphōn sou*), plus the Vulgate & Samaritan Pentateuch (Origen = the Hexapla's LXX column; *verify Qumran
   before asserting it*). "Like Moses" → the uniqueness text is **Numbers 12:6–8** ("mouth to mouth," not
   vision) — the better anchor than Deut 34:10; fulfilled in **Jesus** (John 1:18 — *typological*, present it
   so), not Muhammad (mediated via Gabriel).
4. **Isaiah 21:6–9** (the "rider on a camel"). → context = the **fall of Babylon** (watchmen announce it),
   not future prophets; note the LXX-vs-Masoretic selectivity the speaker flags.
5. **Isaiah 42:1–11** (the Servant / "Kedar"). → Servant = **Israel/Jacob** (Isa 41:8–9), then a Messianic
   individual *from* Israel (Isa 49) — never a Gentile; "Kedar" (42:11) is in a *call to the nations to
   praise*, not the Servant's origin. *(Also the "brings justice to the nations" line → the speaker's
   persecution-stats tangent; see the ⚠ flag.)*
6. **Song of Solomon 5:16** ("*maḥămaddim*"). → *maḥmad* ("desirable") + plural *-im*; *wə-kullo*
   ("altogether") never precedes a name; **no plural-of-majesty for personal proper names** (keep it precise —
   Hebrew *does* have it for common nouns like *Elohim*). **Decisive corroboration: 2 Chronicles 36:19**, same
   word *maḥămaddeha* = "her *precious things*" (the temple vessels) — nobody reads it "her Muhammads."
7. **Isaiah 42:1 "Ahmad" emendation** (Q&A). → the claim that *etmoch* ("I uphold") is a corruption of
   *Ahmad* (Tav→Dalet) fails: the word is followed by a **preposition**, grammatically impossible as a name;
   no textual evidence.
8. **John 16:7,13–14** (the Paraclete). → Jesus *sends* the Comforter (16:7) — if the Comforter is Muhammad,
   then Jesus = the sender (Allah), self-refuting for the Muslim; and the Comforter *glorifies Jesus* + is the
   Holy Spirit. *(Already on our answer page.)*
9. **Daniel 2** (the statue/stone). → the stone-kingdom arises "**in the days of those [Roman] kings**"
   (Dan 2:44), the "divided kingdom" is *internal* (iron+clay = intermarriage), and *olam* = "permanent" — so
   a caliphate abolished in **1924** can't fulfill it; the kingdom of God is a *different kind* of kingdom
   (spiritual), not another layer in the statue. *(Precision: "in the days of those kings" is the *widely-held*
   reading, not the only one — dispensationalists posit a future revived Rome; either way it excludes a 7th-c.
   caliphate. The word is **Aramaic** *ʿalam* here, not Hebrew *olam*.)* **Scholarly leads (all real):**
   **Aubrey Buster & John Walton**, *The Book of Daniel* (NICOT, Eerdmans — verified, recent); **N. T. Wright**
   (four-kingdoms → kingdom-of-God thrust is safely his — *but pin the exact locus before attributing the Dan
   2:44 clause to him*); **Sacha Stern** (*Time and Process in Ancient Judaism*) — but Stern means *olam* = a
   temporal **horizon/duration**, NOT "eternity," so let Dan 2:44's *context* carry "everlasting," don't
   overstate Stern. Plus a long "when did Rome actually fall?" digression (476 / 1204 / 1453 / 1806 / 1924) —
   interesting but a rabbit-trail.

  Jonathan Fox / Grim & Finke / Steven Fish to argue "Islam doesn't bring justice to the nations" (against
  Isaiah 42) is **not the "is Muhammad in the Bible" question**, it generalizes about ~1.8 billion people, and

  is that *denial of religious freedom* (not "extremist religious ideas") drives persecution — they
  **explicitly reject** the clash-of-civilizations framing; and Fish's *Are Muslims Distinctive?* is largely a
  **corrective to stereotypes** (finds Muslims **not** more prone to political violence, **lower** homicide),
  so citing only his voice/accountability finding is cherry-picking. **Do not port this tangent into any
  content** — it fails both the off-topic test and the "steelman accurately, don't cherry-pick" standard.
- **EXPLICIT-VERDICT, but the honest verdict is bounded.** Landing "Muhammad is not the biblical prophet /
  the specific proof-texts fail" in our own voice is correct and matches our answer page. But the "Biblical
  Prophet Dilemma" *frame* ("Islam is false EITHER WAY, case closed") overclaims: Horn 2 assumes the Muslim
  grants the Bible's *theological content* as authoritative, which a Muslim invoking 7:157 need not do (he
  invokes a *prediction*, not the surrounding doctrine). State the frame as an internal pressure, not a
  checkmate — and steelman the Muslim's escape (that 7:157 points to a *bare prediction* extractable from an
  otherwise-corrupted text) before answering.
- **Manuscripts = preservation, not truth**, and the Deut-18 "from among you in Origen/Vulgate" point is a
  *preservation* claim (the qualifier isn't a late insertion) — keep it there.
