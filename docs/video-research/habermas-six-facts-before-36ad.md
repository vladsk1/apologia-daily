## Thesis (our words)
Habermas's controlling claim: **"If the resurrection of Jesus is true, Christianity is true. Period."**
So the pastoral frame is *major in the gospel* (deity, atoning death, bodily resurrection) and don't let
peripheral puzzles (the two Judas-death accounts, etc.) do the work an emotional drift really does — the
gospel's truth doesn't hang on harmonising every detail. The historical core: he takes a **6-year window**
after the crucifixion (30–36 AD, or 33–39 AD on the later-crucifixion date) and argues **six facts** can be
placed *inside* it by "doing history the way the critics do it" — three criteria: **(1) early, (2)
eyewitness, (3) multiple independent sources** (where Matthew–Mark–Luke count as *one* source-stream, John
a second, Acts a third, a Pauline epistle a fourth). The payoff: a fully-formed gospel of a *risen, divine*
Jesus is demonstrably in place within ~5 years of the cross — too early to be a late legendary overlay.

## Argument map — the "six facts" (three sets of two) → primaries to VERIFY
Habermas groups them **two + two + two.** (All Scripture refs below are ESV-checkable and, where noted,
already live on-site.)

**Set 1 — the earliest appearances.**
1. **Appearance to the Twelve** — mostly *group* appearances (relevant against hallucination theory, which
   doesn't do groups). → **Primaries:** 1 Cor 15:5; the individual-appearance minority (Mary, John 20;
   Peter, Luke 24) named only to show how *few* are to individuals.
2. **Appearance to James, the brother of Jesus** — who was a *sceptic* before (Mark 3:21, 31–35; Mark 6:3;
   John 7:5 — the brothers "did not believe"; in Mark 3 the family thinks him "beside himself"). By Acts
   1:14 (the upper-room list of ~120, "Mary and his brothers") James is *among the believers*. → **Primaries:**
   1 Cor 15:7 (the only place the James appearance is recorded); Acts 1:14; the unbelief texts above.
   → **Minor non-canonical lead (footnote-level only):** a fragment of the **Gospel of the Hebrews**
   describing the risen Jesus appearing to James — *non-canonical, 2nd-century, fragmentary; **preserved by
   Jerome, *De Viris Illustribus* 2** (verified). It is **late and text-critically secondary** — usable at
   most as a corroborating footnote, NOT as independent early attestation; Habermas himself flags it as
   non-orthodox.*

**Set 2 — from oral proclamation to fixed creed.**
3. **The earliest oral preaching** — Habermas's Greek-word hook (ASR-mangled as "monogan/logia"): the
   *homologia* (confession) / the single-saying *kerygma* preached "immediately" after the appearances.
   Content = **deity, (atoning) death, (bodily) resurrection.** → **Lead:** the *homologia* word-study; the
   Acts kerygmatic summaries (cf. the sibling note's Acts 1–5, 10, 13, 17).
4. **The crystallised creeds** — that oral confession hardened into memorisable creedal formulae embedded in
   the letters. → **Primaries:** **1 Cor 15:3–7** (the flagship); **Romans 10:9** ("Jesus is Lord");
   **Romans 1:3–4**; **Philippians 2:6–11** (the "hymn"; *morphē* + the Isaiah "every knee" quotation).
   Habermas: many creeds datable to **the 30s AD**; ~80% of NT creedal passages concern the gospel message.
   *(Both figures are popular-level — attribute, don't adopt; the ~80% is not independently verifiable.)*

**Set 3 — Paul closes the chain.**
5. **Paul's conversion** — on critical grounds ~2–3 years after the cross (so ~32–33 AD on a 30 AD cross);
   the Jesus-Seminar dating even puts the 1 Cor 15 creed's *formation* **before Paul's Damascus road**. Paul
   is *both* early *and* an eyewitness (a point skeptics grant). → **Primaries:** Acts 9 (Damascus road;
   the talk's "Acts 9:22–26" is loose — the conversion is Acts 9:1–19); Gal 1:13–16 (Paul the persecutor);
   1 Cor 15:8–9.
6. **Paul's fact-finding visit to Jerusalem** — ~3 years post-conversion (~5–6 yrs post-cross) Paul goes up
   and spends 15 days with **Peter and James**. The verb in **Gal 1:18** is *historeō* (ἱστορέω, the root of
   "history") — "to visit for the purpose of *inquiry*," i.e. Paul checked the gospel with the eyewitnesses.
   Gal 2:2 (a later visit) "lest I should be running in vain"; Gal 2:9 the "right hand of fellowship"; and
   1 Cor 15:11 ("whether I or they, so we preach") ties Paul's message to the eyewitnesses'. → **Primaries:**
   **Gal 1:18–19** (the *historeō* clause) — *the single most reusable lead in the talk*; Gal 2:2, 2:9;
   1 Cor 15:11. **Note: `earlycreed.html` and `paulconv.html` already use Gal 1:18 + *historeō*.**

**Cumulative payoff:** all six sit inside ~5–6 years of the cross; the risen-and-divine-Jesus gospel is
early + eyewitness + multiply-attested. Close: first-century sources exist for the **martyrdoms** of Paul,
Peter, and James — they *died for* what they claimed to have witnessed (evidence of sincerity, not of the
resurrection's truth directly). → **Verified sources:** James via **Josephus, *Ant.* 20.200** (~93 CE,
reporting his ~62 CE stoning); Peter & Paul via **1 Clement 5** (~95 CE). *⚠ Honest caveat: "first-century
source" holds for the **fact** of each death, but 1 Clement 5 does **not** give the **manner** (Peter's
crucifixion / Paul's beheading come from later 2nd-c. sources) — so claim the fact, not the manner. Also
Josephus says James was **stoned**, not the temple-pinnacle/club version of Hegesippus — don't lean on the
dramatic account.*

## Named-scholar LEADS (ASR-mangled names de-mangled; verify EACH — see 2026-07-22 citations pass)
- **Bart Ehrman** (ASR "Bartman") — atheist/agnostic NT scholar. Two uses: (a) his 20-page take-down of
  **mythicists** in *Did Jesus Exist?* (they lack accredited positions / scholarly standing); (b) the
  crucifixion is multiply and independently attested — the talk says **"15 independent sources, many outside
  the NT, within 100 years."** *⚠ Verified: this misrepresents Ehrman.* In *Did Jesus Exist?* he argues for
  Jesus's **existence** from ~**7 independent Gospel-tradition sources plus Paul**, and he **downplays** the
  extra-biblical witnesses (treats Tacitus as hearsay, Josephus as repeating Christian claims). So both the
  "15" and the "many outside the NT" are wrong for Ehrman — that large, extra-biblical-heavy aggregation is
  closer to **Habermas/Licona's own** tally. Do **not** attribute it to Ehrman. Also: Ehrman calls Paul's
  Peter/James visit "**as close to eyewitness testimony as we can get**" — verify exact wording before quoting.
- **Gerd Lüdemann** (ASR "Garrett Ludman") — *atheist German NT scholar*, Jesus-Seminar voter; concedes the
  disciples' resurrection *experiences* and that proclamation began **"immediately."** Concedes the
  *experience/earliness*, **not** the resurrection. *(Already used in `earlycreed.html` — reuse that framing.)*
- **John Dominic Crossan** (ASR "Dom Crossson") — **co-founder / co-chair of the Jesus Seminar** (with
  Robert Funk). Concedes the crucifixion: *"I take it absolutely for granted [that] Jesus was crucified
  under Pontius Pilate"* — **verified genuine; source is Crossan, *The Historical Jesus* (1991), p. 372**
  (NOT *Jesus: A Revolutionary Biography* — that book has a *different* genuine line, "as sure as anything
  historical can ever be," p. 145; don't conflate them).
- **Marcus Borg** — **a prominent *fellow* of the Jesus Seminar, NOT a "co-founder"** (the talk's "co-founder"
  is wrong; the Seminar was founded by **Robert Funk**, 1985, with Crossan). Concedes on historical grounds
  that Jesus was a **healer/exorcist** — a concession of a *historical core*, not of deity. *(Same correction
  as the sibling note.)*
- **Josephus** — named as a Pharisee describing how Pharisees studied (colour for Paul's training); also the
  earliest source for **James's martyrdom (*Antiquities* 20.200, ≈62 AD)** — see the sibling note.

## Guardrail fit (calibration flags — read before pulling anything)
- **"Nobody's close in the history of religions… nobody touches our evidence."** Classic popular-level
  triumphalism. **Cut it.** Our mission line: confidence must be *earned by truth*, never hype (a believer
  built on "we have more facts than anybody" is brittle). State the actual, strong, bounded case.
- **Jesus Seminar "reject 91% of the red-letter words of Jesus."** *⚠ Overstated/ASR.* The verifiable figure
  from *The Five Gospels* is ~**18% of sayings** judged authentic (red+pink) → ~82% not, **not 91%.** Use the
  real number or say "the large majority."
- **morphē (Phil 2:6) "first definition means sharing the same nature."** *⚠ Lexical overstatement.* Standard
  lexica render *morphē* "form / outward appearance that embodies inner reality"; the *nature/essence*
  implication is a *defensible commentator's inference* (Fee, O'Brien, Hawthorne), **not** the dictionary's
  "first definition." Our `library/phil2.html` already argues this carefully — **defer to the essay's calibrated
  wording; do not adopt "the first definition of morphē is 'same nature'."**
- **"Six facts before 36 AD" is a rhetorical frame, not six independent *evidences*.** Fact 1 (crucifixion)
  he explicitly *grants* rather than argues; several "facts" are really one datum (the creed) viewed from
  different angles. Present it as a *timeline/earliness* demonstration, which is its real force — not as "six
  proofs."
- **Resurrection rule (house guardrail):** lead with the **1 Cor 15:3–7 creed**; our voice dates it **~2–5
  years**; **"within months" is attributed to Dunn only.** Keep martyrdom = evidence of the witnesses'
  *sincerity*, never a direct proof of the resurrection.
- **Manuscripts-≠-truth rule:** N/A here (this is *dating of tradition*, not textual transmission).
