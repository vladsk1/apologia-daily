## Thesis (our words)
The **Minimal Facts** method: argue the resurrection **only from data conceded by the vast majority (90–100%)
of critical scholars who specialise in the field** — so the case does not depend on the Bible being inspired
or even reliable. Habermas's framing: *whichever* view of the text you hold (inspired / reliable / unreliable),
the historical data still land you at the resurrection. He runs it from **two texts everyone grants are
genuinely Paul's — 1 Corinthians 15 and the end of Galatians 1 / start of Galatians 2** — because Paul is
early, an eyewitness, and universally accepted as the author of these letters (even far-left critics grant
6–8 Pauline letters). The payoff: a fully-formed gospel of a *risen, divine* Jesus is demonstrably in place
within ~5 years of the cross, and the disciples' post-death experiences are conceded even by skeptics.

## Argument map (beats → primaries to VERIFY)
1. **"You're just using the New Testament" is not an objection.** Every *specialist* uses 1 Cor + Galatians;
   the data for their Pauline authorship + dating are that strong. → **Leads:** the atheist **Michael Martin**
   (*The Case Against Christianity*) conceding **Paul is the best/only eyewitness source** for a resurrection
   appearance (= 1 Cor 15); Ehrman granting 6–8 Pauline letters. *(Both are "hostile-witness" concessions —
   strong, but state them precisely: they concede Paul's *testimony/earliness*, not the resurrection.)*
2. **Ancient historiography sets the bar.** "+65 years is too late" ignores how ancient history works. → **Leads
   (source-DISTANCE, not manuscript-count — keep them distinct!):** *Alexander* (d. 323 BC) — the best-surviving,
   fullest narratives are **Arrian (~AD 140) & Plutarch (~AD 100), ~400+ yrs later**, and **every contemporary
   account is lost** (Callisthenes/Ptolemy/Aristobulus survive only as fragments); *(precision: Diodorus Siculus
   ~60–30 BC and Curtius Rufus ~mid-1st-c. AD are somewhat **earlier** than Arrian/Plutarch though less
   reliable — so say "best surviving narratives ~400 yrs later, contemporaries all lost," not "earliest are
   Arrian/Plutarch")*. *Tiberius* (d. AD 37) — 4 principal sources: Velleius Paterculus (contemporary but a
   short panegyric, "least usable"), **Tacitus & Suetonius ~+80, Cassius Dio ~+185**; **Suetonius** notes 5 of
   the 12 Caesars "later deified" (Julius, Augustus, Claudius, Vespasian, Titus); ancient *bioi* routinely carry
   miracles/portents. **⚠ This is the SOURCE-DISTANCE argument, NOT the discredited "more NT manuscripts than
   Caesar" manuscript-count argument** (the site already retired that count — keep this version, which compares
   *time-gap to earliest narrative*, and don't let it drift into manuscript tallies). Also the genre point:
   **Gospels as Greco-Roman biography** (lead: **Richard Burridge, *What Are the Gospels?*, 1992** — a NT scholar
   *with* classical training [King's College London], not a professional classicist; the book overturned the
   older form-critical "the Gospels are *sui generis*" consensus — Burridge set out to *disprove* the biography
   thesis and was persuaded the other way).
3. **1 Cor 15:1–2 — the gospel Paul *preached at Corinth*.** Content of "the gospel" wherever defined =
   **deity, (atoning) death, (bodily) resurrection.** → **Primaries:** 1 Cor 15:1–2; the *pistis*/"believe" =
   trust-surrender word-note.
4. **1 Cor 15:3–7 — a pre-Pauline CREED Paul *received*.** "I delivered… what I also received" = technical
   handing-on of tradition (cf. 1 Cor 11:23). The appearance list: Peter, the Twelve, 500+, James, "all the
   apostles," Paul. → **Primaries:** 1 Cor 15:3–8, 11; cf. 1 Cor 11:23. **Dating anchor:** Paul *preached* it at
   Corinth ~51–52 AD — the **Gallio / Delphi inscription** fixes **Gallio's proconsulship of Achaia to ~AD 51–52**,
   which (with Acts 18:12, Paul hauled before Gallio) **anchors** Paul's Corinth stay — *say it dates Gallio and
   anchors Paul, not that it "dates Paul" directly. Net-new lead; not yet on-site.*
5. **Galatians 1–2 — Paul got it early, from the eyewitnesses.** Conversion ~+2; Arabia 3 yrs; then the
   Jerusalem visit ~35 AD (~+5), **15 days with Peter and James** (Gal 1:18–19). The verb is *historeō*
   (ἱστορέω, root of "history") — "to visit for firsthand **inquiry**" (two word-studies conclude this: **G. D. Kilpatrick 1959** [in the T. W. Manson memorial vol.] and **J. D. G. Dunn, *NTS* 28 (1982)**). Gal 2:1–2 (later visit; Koester dates
   ~48 AD) "lest I run in vain"; Gal 2:9 the "right hand of fellowship"; the pillars "**added nothing to me**";
   tied to 1 Cor 15:11 ("whether I or they, so we preach"). → **Primaries:** Gal 1:18–19; 2:1–2, 9; 1 Cor 15:11.
   *(**Already on-site:** `earlycreed`/`paulconv` use Gal 1:18 + *historeō*.)*
6. **Push the creed back to ~30 AD.** If Peter/James *gave* it to Paul (~35 AD), they held it earlier; oral
   creedal form takes time to crystallise → back to the cross. → **Leads:** Hurtado, Bauckham, Dunn (deity +
   resurrection "coming out of the gate"); **Dunn's "within months"** (attribute to Dunn ONLY; our voice =
   "~2–5 years"). C. H. Dodd's "more than the weather" line on the 15 days.
7. **The skeptical consensus has moved.** → **Concession leads:** **Dale Allison** ("no doubt the disciples saw
   him"; empty tomb "by a short margin"); **E. P. Sanders** (*The Historical Figure of Jesus* — the disciples'
   post-death seeing is "a given"); **Ehrman** ("I have no objection" to "the earliest followers had experiences
   they believed were of the risen Jesus"); **Howard Clark Kee** ("take it into a court of law"); **Hans von
   Campenhausen** (1 Cor 15:3ff gives "everything you could ask for from an ancient historical text"). Empty
   tomb now conceded by ~**2/3–3/4** of relevant scholars (Habermas's survey figure).
8. **Naturalistic theories are a minority.** Habermas's survey: ~25% of writers offer a naturalistic
   alternative (mostly *non*-specialists, BA-level); among specialists ~10%. A bare "I don't believe it" is a
   *denial*, not a theory. → *(Survey figures are Habermas's own head-count — attribute, don't adopt as hard data.)*

## Named-scholar LEADS (ASR-mangled names de-mangled; verify EACH — see 2026-07-22 citations pass)
- **Michael Martin** — atheist philosopher (*The Case Against Christianity*, 1991); concedes Paul is the **only
  *contemporary eyewitness* account** of a post-resurrection appearance (1 Cor 15:8) — *use that exact scope,
  "only contemporary eyewitness," not a looser "best source."* *Net-new (not on-site).*
- **Dale Allison** — *Resurrecting Jesus* (2005); as an undecided/skeptical historian he grants the disciples
  had experiences they took to be the risen Jesus, and judges the empty-tomb case *slightly* stronger than its
  denial. *"By a short margin" is a paraphrase — verify the verbatim page before quoting.* *Already on-site*
  (appearances/minimalfacts/respred/jewishness).
- **E. P. Sanders** — *The Historical Figure of Jesus* (title, not "Human Figure"); the post-death seeing as a
  historical "given." *Verify whether cited on-site by name.*
- **Bart Ehrman** (ASR "Bartman") — grants 6–8 Pauline letters; "no objection" to the disciples'
  experience-claim. *(Carry the standing correction from the other notes: do NOT attribute a "15 extra-biblical
  sources" crucifixion tally to Ehrman — that's Habermas/Licona's aggregation.)*
- **Hurtado / Bauckham / Dunn** — the creed's deity+resurrection content to ~30 AD; **"within months" = Dunn
  only** (*Jesus Remembered* 854–55). *(All three already verified in the early-high-christology note.)*
- **C. H. Dodd** — the "15 days… more than the weather" remark on Gal 1:18. *(Dodd already verified there.)*
- **Oscar Cullmann** — *The Earliest Christian Confessions* (Bauckham's recommended standard work; **French/
  Alsatian**, not German — carry the correction from the first note).
- **Helmut Koester** (ASR "Helmet Caster") — dates the Gal 2 visit ~48 AD.
- **Hans von Campenhausen** — German church historian; on 1 Cor 15:3ff. *Habermas's "everything you could ask
  for" is a **paraphrase**; the verifiable wording is* "This account meets all the demands of historical
  reliability that could possibly be made of such a text" *(ET, "The Events of Easter and the Empty Tomb," in*
  Tradition and Life in the Church*) — quote that, flag the other as paraphrase.* *Net-new.*
- **Howard Clark Kee** — *What Can We Know About Jesus?* (Cambridge); "court of law." **Carry the correction: Kee
  was a public *critic* of the Jesus Seminar, not a member** — here he's "representing the agnostic viewpoint in
  a debate," which is compatible, but don't tag him "Jesus Seminar."
- **Richard Burridge** — *What Are the Gospels? A Comparison with Graeco-Roman Biography* (**1992**, Cambridge
  UP, SNTSMS 70; King's College London); Gospels as Greco-Roman *bioi*. **NT scholar with classical training,
  not a professional classicist.** *Net-new by name (the concept may appear on-site; the name doesn't).*
- **Two *historeō* (Gal 1:18) word-studies** concluding "firsthand inquiry / investigate" — *identify the
  actual authors before citing (lead: G. D. Kilpatrick 1959; discussed by Dunn).*

## Guardrail fit (calibration flags — read before pulling anything)
- **The 90–100% / "95–97%" consensus figures + the 3,300-source bibliography** are Habermas's own head-counts.
  Genuinely useful, but **attribute them to Habermas's survey; don't launder them into our own voice as hard
  data.** (Same discipline the site already applies to the empty-tomb ~75% figure.)
- **Source-distance vs. manuscript-count.** ⚠ The Alexander/Tiberius comparison is fine as a **time-to-earliest-
  narrative** argument; it must NOT slide into the retired "more manuscripts than Caesar/Plato" count (the site
  already reconciled/dropped that). And even the source-distance point concedes *early attestation*, not that
  the content is *true* — hold the "manuscripts/attestation ≠ truth" guardrail.
- **Hostile-witness concessions are strong but bounded.** Martin/Sanders/Allison/Ehrman/Kee concede the
  *disciples' experiences / the data's earliness*, **not** the resurrection or Christ's deity. State the
  concession at exactly its real weight (neutrality gate: no unearned symmetry, no inflated concession).
- **Resurrection rule (house guardrail):** lead with the **1 Cor 15:3–7 creed**; our voice dates it **~2–5
  years**; **"within months" is Dunn only.**
- **"90% of Jesus's audiences were illiterate," "5 scholars believed the empty tomb in the '70s," etc.** are
  round popular-level claims — verify or soften before use.
