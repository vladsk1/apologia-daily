## Thesis (our words)
The NT epistles quote **pre-existing creeds/confessions** (Paul's "I delivered… what I also received," 1 Cor
11:23; 15:1) that predate the books containing them and date to the **30s AD** — so the church's core
Christology is not a late development. And "**the earliest christology is the highest christology**": the
oldest layers already confess Jesus's deity (worship, forgiving sins, sharing God's throne and the divine
name), not a low Christology that evolved upward.

## Argument map (beats → all already certified on-site / in note #1)
- **Creeds predate their books** — the "delivered/received" formula (1 Cor 11:23; 15:1), broken Greek syntax
  + cadence marking an embedded confession; **C. H. Dodd** (*The Apostolic Preaching*, 1936, on the Acts
  sermon-summaries) and **Oscar Cullmann** (*The Earliest Christian Confessions*) as the founding studies.
  ~15 major creeds, mostly in the epistles; Acts sermon-summaries in Acts 1–5, 10 (Petrine), 13 (Pauline).
- **1 Cor 15:3–7** — the flagship creed; dated to **30–33 AD** by **Gerd Lüdemann** (atheist) and **Pinchas
  Lapid** (non-Christian Jewish NT scholar, ~9 formal reasons it's a creed); "died *for our sins*" =
  substitutionary hint; the longest + earliest appearance list; 1 Cor 15:11 ("whether they or I, so we
  preach") = shared message.
- **The deity creeds** ("earliest = highest"; the **High Christology Club** = Hengel, Hurtado, Dunn,
  Bauckham): **Rom 10:9** ("Jesus is Lord") + **10:13** quoting Joel ("calls on the name of the Lord" =
  *Jehovah*) — *the exact Rom 10→Joel move we already added to `jesus_as_god_nt.html`*; **Rom 1:3–4**;
  **Phil 2:6–11** → Isaiah 45 (God won't share his glory) — *morphē* = "of the nature of"; **Heb 1:3** ("exact
  representation of God's nature"); **Col 2:9**; **1 Cor 8:6** — Jesus reworked *into* the Shema (the
  **Bauckham–Dunn** debate: "in it" vs "snugged up to it") — *already in our `shema-trinity` brief*.
- **Cullmann on *kyrios*** — "Lord," not "God," is the central/highest deity confession, because *kyrios*
  renders the OT Tetragrammaton (YHWH). *(Already the backbone of note #1 + `jesus_as_god_nt.html`.)*
  **⚠ Caveat:** report this as *Cullmann's view*; the premise that the pre-Christian LXX rendered YHWH as
  *kyrios* is **contested** (several early Jewish Greek mss write the Tetragrammaton in Hebrew/paleo-Hebrew) —
  don't lean on the YHWH→*kyrios* mechanism as established fact.
- **The son-of-man / trial scene** — **Mark 2** (forgiving sins → "blasphemy") and **Mark 14:61–64** (Jesus
  affirms "Son of the Blessed," then applies **Daniel 7:13–14**, coming on the clouds + seated at God's right
  hand → the high priest tears his robes). *Son of man = the exalted Daniel-7 figure, NOT "merely human."*
  *(Exactly note #1 §5(ii); our lead there: Darrell Bock, *Blasphemy and Exaltation in Judaism*.)*
- **Objections answered:** legendary-development / "John is late (95 AD)" → the creeds are 30s; the ancient-
  source-distance comparison (Alexander: Arrian/Plutarch ~425–450 yrs later); the "*I received* = Paul's third-
  heaven download" objection → 2 Cor 12 is likely an NDE at Lystra and Paul says it's *unlawful to speak of*,
  so it can't be the creed he proclaims nonstop; illiteracy (70–90%) → creeds as memorizable oral formulae.

## Genuinely net-new references (verify — recorded as leads only)
- **Pinchas Lapide** (1922–1997; **Austrian-born**, non-Christian Orthodox Jew who defended the
  resurrection's historicity), *The Resurrection of Jesus: A Jewish Perspective* (ET 1983) — the formal
  characteristics of the 1 Cor 15 creed; a *hostile-witness* lead. *(Soften Habermas's "German NT doctorate";
  the "~9 characteristics" exact count is unconfirmed.) Not currently cited on-site — a possible addition to
  `earlycreed.html`.*
- **Vernon H. Neufeld**, *The Earliest Christian Confessions* (Eerdmans, 1963) — **published in Bruce
  Metzger's NTTS series** (a Princeton dissertation; *"written under Metzger" as supervisor is unconfirmed —
  say "in Metzger's series"*); a rigorous study of the NT *homologia*. *A lead for `earlycreed.html`.*
- **Habermas & Benjamin C. Shaw's republication of Cullmann's *The Earliest Christian Confessions*** (Wipf &
  Stock, ISBN 978-1-5326-5336-0) — **confirmed**; a cheap in-print edition of the classic (the original is
  scarce/expensive). A citable edition.
- **Romans 4:25** ("raised for our justification") named as a creed — **widely regarded** as a pre-Pauline
  creedal couplet, but **disputed** (Michael Bird argues Paul composed it); present as "widely regarded," not
  settled.
