## Thesis (our words)
Against the Muslim claim that the Qur'an is *perfectly* preserved "letter for letter, word for word," the
speaker argues from Islam's **own** most authoritative hadith (Bukhari, Muslim) that: the Qur'an had **no
collected book in Muhammad's lifetime**; it was compiled *after his death* by fallible men; some material was
**held by only one man** or **forgotten** (whole surahs); companions **differed** over the wording so sharply
that Uthman **burned** the variant copies; and revered companions (esp. **Ibn Masud**) recited verses
differently from — and rejected — the standard text. Conclusion: the *perfect-preservation slogan* is false on
Islam's own testimony. **(Our certified framing is more disciplined — see the calibration flag.)**

## Argument map (beats → primaries to VERIFY — most already in the certified essay/brief)
1. **No book in Muhammad's lifetime; a post-mortem compilation.** → **Sahih al-Bukhari 4986**: after the
   Battle of **Yamama** many *qurra* (memorizers) die; Umar fears "a large part of the Qur'an may be lost";
   Abu Bakr calls collecting it *bidʿa* (an innovation Muhammad never did) before relenting; **Zayd ibn
   Thabit** gathers it "from palm stalks, thin white stones, and the men who knew it by heart," finding the
   **last two verses of Surah al-Tawba (9:128–129)** with only **Abu Khuzayma al-Ansari**. *(Already in the
   essay.)*
2. **Uthman's standardization + the burning.** → **Sahih al-Bukhari 4987**: **Hudhayfa** warns of recitation
   differences "before they differ about the Book like the Jews and Christians"; Uthman's committee copies
   Hafsa's manuscript, writes it in the **Quraysh dialect** on disagreement, and **burns** the other copies.
   *(Already in the essay — the heavily-covered core.)*
3. **Whole surahs forgotten.** → **Sahih Muslim 1050** (Abu Musa al-Ashʿari): a surah once ~as long as Surah
   9 now lost but for *"if the son of Adam had two valleys of wealth he would want a third… nothing fills his
   belly but dust"*; another (like the *Musabbihat*) lost but for *"O you who believe, why do you say what
   you do not do…"* (that surviving line ≈ present-day **Q 61:2**) — the fuller surahs not in today's Qur'an.
   **Net-new lead vs the essay.**
4. **An abrogated-recitation verse still recited at Muhammad's death.** → **Sahih Muslim 1452** (Aisha, "the
   verse of suckling"): "ten sucklings" → abrogated to "five," which "were recited as part of the Qur'an"
   when the Prophet died — yet neither is in the text today. Used to argue *naskh al-tilawa* (abrogation of
   recitation) can't rescue this, since abrogation requires the living Prophet. **Net-new lead.**
5. **A revered companion's variant + rejection of the standard text.** → **Sahih al-Bukhari 4944**: **Ibn
   Masud** read Q **92:3** as *"and the male and the female"* (not "and Him who created the male and the
   female"); **Abu Darda** affirms the Prophet recited it that way *(Bukhari 4944; also Muslim 824)*; + the
   *"take the Qur'an from four"* hadith naming **Ibn Masud first** *(Bukhari 4999)* — yet Ibn Masud was **left
   out** of Uthman's committee and objected ("conceal your codices") *(that objection is **Tirmidhi 3104**,
   hasan sahih — NOT Bukhari)*. **Net-new lead.**
6. **The *sabʿa aḥruf* / dialect rebuttal** (Q&A). → the **Hisham b. Hakim** hadith *(Bukhari 4992; Muslim
   818)* (Umar seizes him for reciting al-Furqan differently; "revealed in seven *aḥruf*"); the speaker's
   point that Umar & Hisham were **both Qurayshi** (different clans — Umar of Banu ʿAdi, Hisham of Banu Asad),
   so "just dialect" can't neatly explain a difference serious enough to nearly come to blows; + Jay Smith's
   point that written dialectal differences need **vowel-pointing** (absent in the 7th c.).
7. **Replies the speaker pre-empts** (steelman these properly — he mostly mocks them): *naskh al-tilawa*
   (abrogation of recitation); the *sabʿa aḥruf* as legitimate variant "modes"; **Abu Khuzayma's "witness of
   two"** (a Sahaba's testimony counts double); the Quranist "hadith-are-false" move (answered with "then
   *which* Qur'an?"). Our essay/brief handle these with the charity the video lacks.

## Guardrail fit (READ FIRST — this topic is high-stakes and our house framing is stricter than the video's)
- **CALIBRATION — the central flag.** Our certified brief/essay explicitly **discipline** this argument:
  *"Do NOT claim the Qur'an is wildly corrupted — that is false and uncharitable; by the standards of ancient
  literature it is a strikingly stable text. The point is narrower and fairer: the claim of PERFECT,
  letter-for-letter preservation is complicated by Islam's OWN most authoritative sources."* The video leans
  hard into "**corruption!**" and "entire chapters gone" as if the Qur'an were textually unreliable. **Keep
  our narrow, fair claim; don't inherit the overstatement.**
- **Don't inflate the variants into "doctrinal splits."** The speaker claims the recitation differences were
  "doctrinal / could split the religion." Most *qira'at* differences are minor (vocalization, synonyms); Ibn
  Masud's 92:3 is a real but limited variant. State what each datum actually shows, not a maximal "the
  religion was doctrinally fractured."
- **Steelman the Muslim replies.** *Naskh al-tilawa*, the *sabʿa aḥruf*, and the "witness of two" are the
  real Muslim answers — present them at their strongest **before** weighing them (the `apologia-neutrality`
  gate requires it). The video dismisses them as "just words"; we don't.
- **⚠ One weak narration in the Q&A — do NOT cite as authentic.** The "**Uthman saw errors (*laḥn*) and said
  leave them, the Arabs will fix it with their tongues**" report is **not** in the Sahih collections — it's in
  Ibn Abi Dawud's *Kitab al-Masahif* with a **broken/mursal isnad** (the ʿIkrima→Uthman link), and classical
  + modern scholars reject it (grammarians defend the readings). It is the **weakest link** in the lecture and
  an easy Muslim rebuttal — if ever used, label it explicitly weak/contested, never Sahih.
- **The inverse of the manuscripts-≠-truth rule.** "The Qur'an isn't *perfectly* preserved" does **not** by
  itself prove the Bible is, or that Christianity is true — those are separate questions (the resurrection
  case carries that load). And apply the same standard evenly: don't over-concede the Bible's own
  transmission is flawless. *(The essay was hardened 2026-07-20 to remove a "comparable transmission
  histories" over-concession — keep that discipline.)*
- **TONE.** The ironic-"Islamophobe" bit and the mockery are a **negative model**; our register is 1 Peter
  3:15 gentleness and respect toward Muslims.
