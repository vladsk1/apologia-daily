## Thesis (our words)
The **Islamic Dilemma** is an *internal* critique run entirely from the Qur'an's own words: the Qur'an
repeatedly, and in the **present tense**, calls the Torah and Gospel God's revelation ("guidance and light,"
5:46), commands 7th-century Jews and Christians to live by the books *in their hands* (5:47, 5:68), calls
itself their "confirmer" (*muṣaddiq*), declares God's words unalterable (6:115), and — the sharpest point —
sends Muhammad himself, "if you are in doubt," to "ask those who have been reading the Scripture before you"
(10:94). The fork: the 7th-century Bible is, by the manuscript evidence, essentially today's Bible, which
contradicts the Qur'an where the Gospel is load-bearing (deity of Christ, crucifixion [4:157 denies it],
resurrection). So either that Bible was already corrupt (and God affirmed/consulted a corrupt book) or it is
reliable (and it refutes the Qur'an). Frost adds an **"accountability argument"**: the Qur'an *commands*
Christians to obey the Gospel on pain of hell (5:47), yet obeying the Gospel means confessing Jesus as Lord —
which the Qur'an calls *shirk*, also punished by hell — an alleged no-win bind.

## Argument map (beats → primaries to VERIFY — most already verified & live in `islam-dilemma.html`)
1. **Why start here.** It's an *internal* critique from the Qur'an (Islam's own highest authority), so it
   sidesteps hadith/sira/tafsir disputes and works for any Muslim; and it's not the aggressive "attack
   Muhammad's character" route. → *This is the sanctioned "shared-premise internal critique" case, NOT
   false common ground (see guardrails).*
2. **The Qur'an affirms the earlier Scriptures — pervasively.** → **Primaries:** Q 3:3; 5:46–48; the ~18
   *muṣaddiq* ("confirming what is with you") verses; 6:115 ("none can alter His words"); + hadith (Bukhari:
   Muhammad judges by the Torah; Abu Dawud: places the Torah on a cushion). **Lead:** **Martin Whittingham**,
   *A History of Muslim Views of the Bible* — the "~119 positive verses" figure. *(All already in our essay.)*
3. **Present-tense commands to obey the books in-hand.** → **Primaries:** Q 5:47 ("People of the Gospel,
   judge by what Allah has revealed therein"); 5:68 ("you stand on nothing until you uphold the Torah, the
   Gospel…"); 5:43 (Torah = "the judgement of Allah"); **10:94** (Muhammad sent to consult the Scripture).
4. **The Bible of the 7th c. = today's Bible.** → **Primaries:** Dead Sea Scrolls (Isaiah); Codices
   Vaticanus & Sinaiticus. *(Manuscripts prove **preservation**, not truth — hold that guardrail.)*
5. **It contradicts the Qur'an where the Gospel is load-bearing.** Deity of Christ (John 1:1; 20:28) vs Q
   4:171; 5:72–73; 9:30; crucifixion (1 Cor 15:3–4) vs **Q 4:157**; resurrection (1 Cor 15:17). Also: Allah
   "not a father" (5:18); *Isa ibn Maryam*, never *ibn Allah*.
6. **Muhammad-in-the-Bible + verify-the-Qur'an leads (weaker; handle carefully).** Q 7:157 (the *ummī*
   prophet "written with them in the Torah and Gospel"); 2:146 / 6:20 ("know him as their sons"); 10:94 as a
   *verification* invitation → the Moses/Pharaoh & Joseph (Q 12 vs Genesis 37) story-divergences.
7. **The standard Muslim replies** (Frost lists them, then dismisses — *our essay steelmans them properly*):
   the "lost original *Injil*"; *tahrif* (corruption); partialism ("only *part* is confirmed"); supersession/
   abrogation; "conscious edits"; "God was being sarcastic." → **Our `islam-dilemma.html` gives the three
   strongest at full strength (original-vs-today's-Bible; *muhaymin*/supersession; *tahrif*-as-meaning) and
   grants that each NARROWS the dilemma — the neutrality-compliant version Frost's lecture lacks.**
8. **Frost's "accountability argument"** (his distinctive addition): command to obey the Gospel (5:47) +
   capacity (2:121; 3:199 say Christians *do* recite/follow it) + obeying it = *shirk* → no-win. *Net-new
   framing vs our essay; see live-door note — promising but the "therefore Allah is evil" horn is too hot.*

## Named-leads / sources (verify — see 2026-07-22 citations pass)
- **Martin Whittingham**, *A History of Muslim Views of the Bible: The First Four Centuries* (**De Gruyter,
  2020** — not OUP) — the standard scholarly anchor; and the caution that textual-corruption charges are
  *older* than the "Ibn Hazm invented it" slogan. *(Already the backbone of our essay.)* **⚠ The "~119
  positive verses" figure is UNVERIFIED as Whittingham's own stated number** — he catalogues the verses in an
  appendix, but the exact "119" could not be confirmed; treat it as a lead, and use the safe general claim
  (the Qur'an speaks positively of the Torah/Gospel in far more verses than negatively).
- **David Wood** (Acts 17 Apologetics / DCCI) — popularized the argument's name; **West/Wesley Huff** (on
  Piers Morgan) — mainstream exposure. *Popularizers, not citable primaries.*
- **George of Bʿeltan (Beltan)** — Frost's "patriarch of Antioch, 8th c., commentary on Matthew" is **REAL
  and correctly identified** (not an ASR garble): West Syriac (Syriac Orthodox) Patriarch of Antioch, r.
  ~758–789/90; the anti-Islamic material is in **ch. 49 of the introduction to his Commentary on Matthew** —
  among the earliest Syriac quotations of the Qur'an and an early response to the *tahrif* charge. *(Keep
  "attributed to" — the attribution/dating of that chapter is debated.)* **Do NOT** swap him for George,
  Bishop of the Arab Tribes (d. 724) — a different George. Additional genuine early witnesses for
  robustness: **John of Damascus** (*De Haeresibus* ~743), **Theodore Abū Qurrah**, the **Timothy I –
  al-Mahdī dialogue** (~781), the **Apology of al-Kindī**.
- **"Avery"** (a friend who presents the 10:94 argument) — not citable.
- **Christopher Frost's book** on the Islamic Dilemma — a copyrighted lead, not a source; our essay already
  cites its existence.

## Q&A leads (from the audience session — additional, all UNVERIFIED)
Mined from the post-lecture Q&A (a few genuinely net-new branches; verify each before any use):
- **Surah 3:55** — "those who follow Jesus will be *uppermost* over the disbelievers to the Day of
  Resurrection." A real dilemma-branch lead (which visible community are "the followers of Jesus"?). **⚠ But
  Frost's reading — "Christians are economically/culturally/technologically dominant, so *we* are the
  uppermost" — is triumphalist and weak; DO NOT USE that gloss** (it's worldly-success-as-proof, and it
  boomerangs historically). The verse is a lead; that particular argument for it is not.
- **Waraqah ibn Nawfal** — Khadija's cousin, a Christian scribe who (per the first-revelation account in
  *Sahih al-Bukhari*) knew/wrote the Gospel in Hebrew (or Arabic). The angle: Islam's *own* earliest
  tradition places a real, written Gospel in Muhammad's own circle — so "the original *Injil* vanished
  without trace" sits awkwardly with Waraqah. A usable lead (verify the Bukhari wording; don't overstate what
  Waraqah "had").
- **Surah 2:79 (+ 2:80)** — the single verse most often cited by Muslims *for* textual corruption ("woe to
  those who write the Scripture with their own hands… for a small price"). Frost's reply: in context (2:75–85)
  it addresses **the Jews**, never names the Torah/Gospel, and 2:80 ("the Fire will not touch us except for a
  *limited number of days*") echoes a **rabbinic tradition (Genesis Rabbah / Talmudic material)**, so 2:79 is
  about a specific Jewish claim, not the corruption of the Bible. **Useful and on-point** (our essay already
  lists 2:75/2:79/3:78 as verses "read by some later exegetes as gesturing toward tampering") — **verify the
  Genesis Rabbah "limited days" parallel** before deploying.
- **The crucifixion + "they killed the prophets" pattern** (Q 4:155, 2:87, 3:181) — the Islamic framing that
  Jesus's attempted killing fits Israel's pattern of rejecting prophets. Standard; already adjacent to
  `answers/did-jesus-die-on-the-cross-islam.html`.

## Guardrail fit (READ before pulling anything — this topic is high-stakes)
- **TONE is the biggest issue.** Frost's register is polemical and at points mocking ("desperation looks
  humorous"; "slightly sadistic I know, but I enjoy seeing the *daʿīs* unable to respond"; repeated "Oops").
  **This violates 1 Peter 3:15 and our house rule flatly. Never replicate it.** Our essay's frame is the
  model: "an honest question pressed with gentleness and respect, not a trap sprung in triumph."
- **FALSE-COMMON-GROUND — this is the *sanctioned exception*.** Per `CLAUDE.md`, "the Qur'an's own praise of
  the earlier Scriptures driving the Islamic Dilemma is a real shared premise, not false common ground." So
  running the internal critique from the Qur'an's affirmation is *legitimate* — but still frame it as "the
  Qur'an's *own* premise," never as "we honor the same Scriptures / the same God."
- **EXPLICIT-VERDICT, but BOUNDED.** Land the verdict in our voice (the internal tension is real and does not
  dissolve) — but bound it exactly as the essay does: the dilemma exposes an *internal tension*, it is **not
  a proof of the Resurrection**, and the strongest reply (supersession/*muhaymin*) *narrows* it. Don't
  overclaim "Islam is definitively false" from this one argument; and drop Frost's "therefore Allah is
  evil/ignorant" flourish (rhetoric, not a bounded conclusion).
- **STEELMAN the replies.** Frost lists the Muslim replies only to ridicule them. Our content must present
  the best ones (esp. supersession + *tahrif*-as-meaning) at full strength *before* answering — the
  `apologia-neutrality` gate requires it.
- **Precision the popular version botches** (our essay already fixes these): distinguish *tahrif al-maʿnā*
  (meaning) from *tahrif al-naṣṣ* (text); don't claim "no Muslim alleged textual corruption before Ibn Hazm"
  (Whittingham: older); manuscripts prove **preservation, not truth**; the *ummī* (7:157) meaning is
  genuinely debated (illiterate / unlettered / Gentile — cf. Günther 2002).
- **Two specific over-reaches to avoid (2026-07-22 citations pass):** (a) **Q 10:94** — the *verse* is
  accurately cited, but the polemical *inference* ("the Qur'an tells Muslims to fact-check with the People of
  the Book") is the **contested step**; Muslim exegetes read it as rhetorical reassurance to Muhammad, not a
  literal instruction. Our essay already lands the verdict *at* 10:94 while granting that reading — mirror
  that, don't assert the inference as obvious. (b) **The dirham/drachma anachronism (Q 12:20)** — etymology
  is right but it's **weak as a knockdown**: it boomerangs (Genesis 37:28 uses generic "pieces of silver"),
  and *dirham* also means a generic weight/coin. Don't use it as a decisive error.
