## Thesis (our words)
A career-retrospective interview: Habermas walks his **12 facts** (6 "minimal facts" + 6 further well-attested
facts) and the shift in resurrection scholarship since the 1970s (Bultmann's Hellenistic-myth paradigm → his
1976 death → the third quest → bodily-resurrection scholarship back in the mainstream), framed around his new
4-volume *On the Resurrection* (Vol. 1 evidences; Vol. 2 alternative theories; Vol. 3 a ~140-question survey
of the whole scholarly landscape; Vol. 4 application/theology/grief).

## Argument map (beats → primaries — ALL already certified on-site; leads flagged where net-new)
- **The 6 minimal facts:** (1) Jesus died by crucifixion; (2) the disciples had experiences they believed
  were appearances of the risen Jesus; (3) they were transformed / willing to die; (4) the resurrection was
  proclaimed very early (the creeds); (5) the conversion of the sceptic **James**; (6) the conversion of the
  enemy **Paul**. The other six (incl. **burial** and the **empty tomb**) are "further facts" that pass the
  evidence test but not his ~95%-of-scholars bar. *(All in `minimalfacts.html` + the briefs.)*
- **His THREE strongest arguments** (same as the prior notes): **(1) Galatians 1–2** — Paul's Jerusalem visit
  interviewing Peter & James (Ehrman: "how much closer to the eyewitnesses do we get?"); **(2) the creedal
  argument** — 1 Cor 15:3–7 and the many NT creeds dating to the **30s AD**; **(3) the criteria** (eyewitness,
  early, enemy attestation, multiple attestation). *(All certified in `earlycreed`/`paulconv`/`minimalfacts`.)*
- **Fact 1 detail — cause of death.** The medical study (majority medical view = **asphyxiation**, distinct
  from suffocation), the Roman sources (spear-thrust, breaking the ankles), and **David Strauss's** death-blow
  to the swoon theory ("a half-dead Jesus couldn't convince anyone he was the risen Lord of life"). → *Baylor
  medical article is a net-new reference (see below); Strauss + the Roman sources are standard (already in
  `was-jesus-dead`/`appearances`).*
- **Empty tomb** (§ your linked timestamp ~85 min): the new book lists **~21 arguments**; the best = **women
  as first witnesses** (embarrassment criterion, all 4 gospels), the **Jerusalem factor** (you can't preach a
  risen Jesus minutes from an occupied tomb), and **multiple attestation**. *(All in `emptytomb.html`.)*
- **Bodily resurrection word-study:** *anastasis / egeirō* denote **bodily** raising (never a "wispy" spirit);
  **Philippians 3:21** ("transform our lowly *sōma*" — Paul uses *sōma*, not *pneuma*); Romans 8:11. Leads:
  **N. T. Wright** (*The Resurrection of the Son of God*, 2003), Robert Gundry, Mike Licona, **John Granger
  Cook**. *(Already the backbone of `respred.html`.)*
- **Objections answered:** legendary development (one angel → two is not "mythology abounding"); **Mark 13:30**
  "this generation" (*genea* can mean "race"; Jesus in the same context says he doesn't know the day, 13:32;
  and his central teaching is the kingdom, which the resurrection vindicates). *(Standard.)*

## Guardrail fit (calibration flags — same standing items as the prior Habermas notes)
- **The Ehrman "15 independent sources" claim recurs** (both for the crucifixion and generally). **Carry the
  standing correction** from the other notes: in *Did Jesus Exist?* Ehrman argues from ~7 Gospel-tradition
  sources + Paul and **downplays** the extra-biblical ones — the "15, many outside the NT" aggregation is
  **Habermas/Licona's own**, not Ehrman's. Don't attribute it to Ehrman.
- **Resurrection house rules hold:** lead with the **1 Cor 15:3–7 creed**; our voice dates it **~2–5 years**
  ("within months" = Dunn only); **martyrdom = evidence of the witnesses' sincerity, not a direct proof**
  (Habermas is careful here too: "I read their feet," and he rejects the overreach that we can *prove* the
  disciples were martyred — first-century martyrdom data only for Peter, Paul, James).
- **The Gospel of the Hebrews James-appearance** (~125 AD) — Habermas himself calls it "not trustworthy";
  footnote-level only, never load-bearing (matches our `habermas-six-facts` note).
- **Crossan / Borg crucifixion concessions** are correctly bounded (they concede the *crucifixion*, not the
  resurrection); Marcus Borg = a **fellow** of the Jesus Seminar, not "co-founder" (carry that correction).
