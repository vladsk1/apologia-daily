## Outreach sequence (per target)

Week 0–2: follow, leave 3–5 genuinely substantive comments, share one of their videos.
Week 2: the GIFT message (no ask). Week 3+: the ask, only if they responded.

**Draft 1 — the gift (micro-tier, e.g. Sechler):**
> Hey [name] — your [specific video] was one of the clearest treatments of [topic] I've seen
> at any channel size. I build Apologia Daily (apologiadaily.com) — every argument on it is
> fact-checked against primary sources and doctrinally reviewed before it ships (we've pulled
> fabricated quotes that circulate everywhere in apologetics). I've set you up a lifetime Pro
> account, zero strings: [code]. If it's ever useful for prep, that's all I hoped for.

**Draft 2 — the ask (only after a warm reply):**
> Glad it's been useful. If you ever felt it was worth mentioning to your audience, I'd set
> you up a referral code — your people get an extended trial, you get 30% of anything they
> spend in year one. And if there's an objection you keep getting in comments, tell me — I'll
> build the fully-sourced answer page for it and you can point people there.

**Draft 3 — book-club angle (Girl Talk):**
> Your weekly group is exactly who we built the Evidence Library for — long-form, fully
> footnoted essays your members can read before each session. Happy to set the whole group up
> with Pro free for a season if you'd like to trial it as a companion resource.

## Comment-section strategy (the answer to "should we DM commenters?")

Cold-DMing strangers who comment on GodLogic etc.: **no** — spam-pattern, ToS risk to our
accounts, ~zero conversion. Instead:
- Answer questions publicly in those comment sections, two gentle sourced sentences, no link;
  profile bio carries the link. Self-selection replaces intrusion.
- When we have the exact `/answers/` page: answer first, then "full sourced case here if
  you want it."
- Video-reply to comments on OUR reels (algorithm boost).
- DM only people who engaged with US first — warm, invited, converts.
