## `answers/isnt-the-trinity-a-contradiction.html`

- **Phrase:** "triangle having three sides" — _About this comparison_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** Only a point about logic: one thing can have a "three-ness" to it without being a contradiction. The form "one X, three Y" isn't nonsense.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not a picture of God. God is not made of three parts the way a triangle has three sides (an error called partialism). The Father, Son, and Spirit are each fully God — not one-third each.

## `ev-s3.html`

- **Phrase:** "the incarnate Son's functional subordination during his earthly ministry" — _"The Father is greater than I" (John 14:28)_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** In the incarnation the Son voluntarily submits to the Father in his earthly mission and human nature; the Father is greater in role and order (sender to sent), not in being.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that the Son is lesser in nature, deity, or eternity. That is the Arian/subordinationist error the Nicene Creed excludes: the Son is "true God from true God," co-equal and co-eternal.
- **Phrase:** "by taking human form" — _"Made himself nothing" (Philippians 2:7)_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** The eternal Son added a true human nature and veiled his glory, taking "the form of a servant" &mdash; the emptying is that humble taking-on, a subtraction of nothing.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that Christ emptied himself of his deity or ceased to be fully God during his earthly life (the kenotic error). He remained fully God while becoming fully man.

## `ev-s6.html`

  - ✅ **Is saying:** The Nicene "God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God" &mdash; the Son eternally receives the one, whole, undivided divine essence from the Father in a timeless relation of origin with no beginning; he is co-equal and co-eternal.
