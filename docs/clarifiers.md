  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that the Father alone is God while Jesus is a lesser &ldquo;Lord.&rdquo; Because &ldquo;Lord&rdquo; here carries YHWH&rsquo;s own name, the verse confesses Christ&rsquo;s full deity, not his subordination.

## `library/trinity_islam.html`

- **Phrase:** "two people who both love the one God" — _Reasoning together &mdash; not &ldquo;same God, different label&rdquo;_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** Muslims and Christians are both monotheists who intend to worship the one Creator, and can reason together honestly and respectfully rather than talk past each other.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that the two faiths&rsquo; accounts of God are the same or equally adequate, nor that the disagreement is small. Whether God is triune, and who Jesus is, is precisely the real question still dividing them &mdash; a genuine difference, not a merely verbal one.

## `library/trinity_jw.html`

- **Phrase:** "the Father in unveiled glory greater than the Son veiled in flesh" — _Greater in rank, not in nature_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** "The Father is greater than I" speaks of position within the incarnation &mdash; the Son, having emptied himself and taken the form of a servant, stands under the Father in the economy of salvation.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that the Son is a lesser or created being (the Watchtower's Arian claim). Jesus says "greater" (meiz&#333;n, rank), not "better" (kreitt&#333;n, nature); in the same Gospel, "I and the Father are one."

## `library/trinity_mormons.html`

- **Phrase:** "deification language is biblical" — _Deified by grace, not gods by nature_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** Scripture and the fathers really do speak of believers becoming "partakers of the divine nature" (2 Peter 1:4) &mdash; glorified creatures drawn by grace into the life of God (theosis).
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not the LDS claim that humans become Gods of the same kind, or that God the Father was once a man. The Creator/creature line is never crossed: we are made partakers, not partners of the same species.

## `library/typology.html`

- **Phrase:** ""without father or mother or genealogy, having neither beginning of days nor end of life"" — _Melchizedek as a type_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** Genesis records no lineage, birth, or death for Melchizedek, and that deliberate silence of the record makes him a fitting portrait of a priest whose office never ends.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** That Melchizedek was an eternal or divine being &mdash; nor that the Son&rsquo;s eternal generation derives from him. The type is only a shadow; the eternal Son casts it, and is not diminished by it.

## `library/uniqueness.html`

- **Phrase:** "nor that he was exalted to heaven as a divine being" — _Defining "resurrection," not denying deity_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** The specific claim under study is a bodily rising, distinguished from a merely spiritual survival or exaltation-of-the-soul reading.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not a denial of Christ's deity, ascension, or heavenly exaltation &mdash; all affirmed across the library. It narrows what "resurrection" asserts, not who Christ is.

## `worldviews.html`

- **Phrase:** "Functional submission within the Godhead's work of salvation" — _Economic submission, not a lesser rank of deity_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** The Son's obedience belongs to the work of salvation and to his true human nature; the same sentence affirms he is co-equal and co-eternal with the Father.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not an eternal subordination of the Son in being, nature, or deity &mdash; the Arian error the creeds exclude. The three persons are co-equal and co-eternal.
- **Phrase:** "it is a coherent one" — _"Coherent" = the reading holds together_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** The Muslim reading of Jesus's Qur'anic titles (Word, Spirit, Messiah) as marks of honor is internally consistent, and we should state it fairly before answering it.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that denying Christ's full deity is warranted or sound. The very next lines press why this one figure is dressed in such unique language; Jesus is God incarnate.
- **Phrase:** "quietly leaning in the same direction" — _A shared word, not a shared doctrine_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** The Qur'an's own words (Surah 19:33) speak of Jesus dying and being raised, which opens a gentle conversation; some Muslim scholars even soften the crucifixion denial.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that the Qur'an teaches Christ's bodily crucifixion and resurrection &mdash; its dominant reading (Surah 4:157) denies the cross, which Scripture makes the hinge of salvation. The overlap is verbal and partial, never doctrinal.
