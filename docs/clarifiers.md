- **Phrase:** "the same God" — _What's conceded, and what isn't_

## `answers/isnt-the-trinity-a-contradiction.html`

- **Phrase:** "triangle having three sides" — _About this comparison_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** Only a point about logic: one thing can have a "three-ness" to it without being a contradiction. The form "one X, three Y" isn't nonsense.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not a picture of God. God is not made of three parts the way a triangle has three sides (an error called partialism). The Father, Son, and Spirit are each fully God — not one-third each.

## `library/eternal_generation.html`

- **Phrase:** "receives the divine nature from the Father" — _Eternal generation, not subordination_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** A relation of origin: the Son is eternally &ldquo;from&rdquo; the Father, begotten not made &mdash; &ldquo;true God from true God.&rdquo;
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that the Son is created, later, or lesser in being (that would be Arianism). The three persons are co-equal and co-eternal; derivation of person is not inequality of essence.

## `library/evil.html`

- **Phrase:** "into God's own being" — _Holding the classical line_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** The Son, who is truly God, entered real human suffering and death on the cross &mdash; God is not a spectator above our pain.
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** Not that the divine nature "dies," nor that the Father suffered (an error called patripassianism). Moltmann presses this further than classical impassibility allows; the guardrail the site affirms is that God the Son suffered in his human nature, while the divine life does not end.

## `library/shema.html`

- **Phrase:** "echad" — _About this word_
  - ✅ **Is saying:** echad is simply the ordinary Hebrew word for &ldquo;one&rdquo;; in context it can describe a unity made of parts (&ldquo;one flesh&rdquo;).
  - ⛔ **Not saying:** It does not by itself prove plurality in God or the Trinity. That &ldquo;compound unity&rdquo; argument is a discredited overreach; the case for the triune God rests on divine identity, not on this one word.
