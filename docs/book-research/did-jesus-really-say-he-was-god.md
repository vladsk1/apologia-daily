## The book's spine

One argument, built **historically from Mark (the earliest Gospel), not from John's explicit "I am"
sayings**, and weighing above all the reaction of Jesus' **enemies** (criterion of embarrassment /
hostile testimony): in **two "blasphemy-accusation" courtroom scenes**, Jesus claimed authority that
his own adversaries recognized as belonging to **God alone** — so the best historical explanation is
that **Jesus claimed divine authority** (he "said he was God" *implicitly but unmistakably*, without
ever needing the slogan "I am God"). This shows **continuity** between Jesus' own self-claims and the
church's later confession of his deity — undercutting the "the church invented it later" story.

**The two scenes (the engine of the whole book):**
1. **The healing of the paralytic — Mark 2:1–12** → Jesus claims **authority to forgive sins on
   earth** (a divine prerogative; the scribes cry blasphemy, **Mark 2:7**).
2. **Jesus' Jewish examination — Mark 14:53–65** → Jesus claims **authority to judge from heaven**
   (Daniel 7 + Psalm 110, **Mark 14:62**; the high priest cries blasphemy).

Together: **authority to forgive on earth + authority to judge from heaven = a claim to divine
authority**, escalating across the two scenes.

## Chapter-by-chapter map (in our words → primary-source leads to verify)

### Front matter
- **Foreword — Darrell L. Bock.** *Lead: Bock is a real mainstream evangelical NT scholar; a
  credibility anchor, not a source to quote from the foreword.*
- **Introduction — "More Than a Man?"** Reframes the churchgoer's "Jesus never claimed to be God"
  historically; sets the **Mark-not-John** strategy (skeptics dismiss John's "I am" sayings as late).
  *Lead: the "State of Theology" survey (many churchgoers now say Jesus was a great teacher, not God)
  — verify the survey (Ligonier/Lifeway) before citing. Cross-map: `ev-m-hist_jesus`.*

### Part One — Person of Interest (historical method)
- **Ch. 1 — Let's Make History.** Can we know the past? Historiography, hermeneutics, the postmodern
  challenge; studying Jesus "like any other ancient figure." *Cross-map: `ev-m-multiatt`.*
- **Ch. 2 — Rules of Evidence.** The toolkit: **argue to the best explanation**, historical "bedrock,"
  **criteria of authenticity**; deliberately **not** appealing to biblical inspiration; names his own
  biases. **⚠️ Note:** ch. 2 is about historical *method*, **not** the 1 Cor 15 creed (an earlier
  inferred draft wrongly tied it there — corrected). *Cross-map: `ev-m-hist_jesus`, `ev-m-earlycreed`.*

### Part Two — First Blasphemy Scene · the paralytic (Mark 2:1–12)
- **Ch. 3 — The Core Scene: A Miraculous Healing.** Jesus' **miracle-worker reputation = "bedrock"**;
  the easily-missed move — he **forgives the man's sins before healing** (Mark 2:5). *Leads: **Mark
  2:1–12**. Cross-map: `library/jesus_claims.html`.*
- **Ch. 4 — A Blasphemy Accusation: Who Can Forgive Sins?** The scribes: "**Who can forgive sins but
  God alone?**" (**Mark 2:7**). Introduces **"third-party" forgiveness** (pronouncing forgiveness for
  sins not committed against oneself — reserved to God); surveys Greco-Roman + Jewish sources for any
  parallel and finds none. *Leads: Mark 2:7; the parallel-search sources (verify which). Cross-map:
  `ev-m-jesus_claims`.*
- **Ch. 5 — A Divine Claim: Authority to Forgive on Earth.** Answers **Hägerland**'s challenge that
  "**the Son of Man has authority on earth to forgive sins**" (**Mark 2:10**) is a later addition /
  narrator's aside — via five questions on plausibility/coherence; the healing publicly backs the
  invisible claim. *Leads: Mark 2:10; Hägerland's actual argument (verify). Cross-map:
  `library/jesus_as_god_nt.html`.*

### Part Three — Second Blasphemy Scene · Jesus' examination (Mark 14:53–65)
- **Ch. 6 — The Core Scene: A Legal Investigation.** The night examination before the high priest and
  council. **✅ Archaeology lead — the Caiaphas ossuary:** a bone-box uncovered near Jerusalem in
  **1990**, inscribed **"Joseph, son of Caiaphas,"** **widely (not universally) identified** as the
  high priest of this scene. *Verify the 1990 find + inscription + the "widely identified" hedge
  (mainstream ID, not absolute) — the demo hedges it correctly. Cross-map: `library/archaeology.html`,
  `library/jesus_as_god_nt.html`. Leads: Mark 14:53–65.*
- **Ch. 7 — Questioning Jesus: Who Do You Think You Are?** The high priest: "**Are you the Christ, the
  Son of the Blessed?**" (**Mark 14:61**); Jesus answers with **Daniel 7 + Psalm 110** — the Son of Man
  seated at God's right hand and coming on the clouds (**Mark 14:62**). Answers the "no eyewitness to a
  private trial" objection: **a source needn't be sympathetic** (guards, servants, hostile witnesses,
  later insiders — Nicodemus, Joseph of Arimathea, even Paul). *Leads: **Mark 14:61–62; Daniel 7:13–14;
  Psalm 110:1**. Cross-map: `ev-m-jesus_as_god_nt`, `ev-m-daniel70`.*
- **Ch. 8 — A Blasphemy Accusation: You Can't Judge Me!** Why was it blasphemy? Answers **Casey**'s
  (and **Ehrman**'s) three-pronged argument that claiming messiahship / citing Daniel's Son of Man
  wasn't blasphemous — Jesus claimed **more**: to **share God's own heavenly throne and authority**.
  **⚠️ ORTHODOXY CLARIFIER (carry this):** "share God's own throne" must be framed as **inclusion within
  the *identity of the one God*** (Bauckham's divine-identity Christology), **NOT a second/lesser deity
  or an exalted deputy** — guard against a "two powers"/subordinationist misread. The demo flagged an
  optional `orthonote` here; use the same "Is-saying / Not-saying" fence on our pages. *Cross-map:
  `ev-m-jesus_claims`; verify Casey/Ehrman's actual argument.*
- **Ch. 9 — A Divine Claim: Authority to Judge in Heaven.** Read against Jewish belief that **God alone
  judges** and **1 Enoch (the Similitudes, chs. 37–71)** where the Son of Man is the heavenly judge —
  Jesus claims a **divine prerogative to judge**. Paired with Mark 2 (forgive on earth), the two scenes
  = one escalating claim. **⚠️ Two flags:** (1) **Ehrman's concession is LITERARY, not historical** —
  he grants that *Mark's narrative* has the high priest treat the answer as blasphemy (a God-only
  claim) **while still denying the historical Jesus said it**; keep that precise framing (the demo
  fixed an overstatement here). (2) **The 1 Enoch Similitudes' date is debated** (likely 1st c. AD but
  contested) — use "illuminates the *concept* of a heavenly Son-of-Man judge," don't over-lean on it as
  fixed pre-Christian background. *Cross-map: `ev-m-daniel70`, `ev-m-phil2`. Leads: 1 Enoch 37–71.*

### Part Four — Battle of the Views (testing the explanations)
- **Ch. 10 — Limited Power: Authority over Some of Reality.** Gathers the **ten data points**; tests
  the **human-authority** hypothesis (prophet/agent given power over *part* of reality), including
  **Kirk's "idealized human"** reading — pictured as a **court bailiff** acting on the judge's behalf
  yet never possessing the judge's own power. Steelmans it fairly, then strains it against the ten
  facts. **⚠️** keep the bailiff image tied to *"that limited-authority view,"* never pull-quotable as
  our own picture of Jesus. *Cross-map: `ev-m-jesus_claims`.*
- **Ch. 11 — Total Power: Authority over All of Reality.** The **divine-authority** hypothesis runs the
  same five criteria and **passes all five (P/P/P/P/P)**, best-explaining all ten facts. Key point:
  Jesus didn't need the words "I am God" — **claiming to forgive on earth AND judge from heaven was
  itself a claim to divine authority.** *Cross-map: `library/jesus_as_god_nt.html`, `ev-m-phil2`.*
- **Ch. 12 — Conclusion: How Jesus Said He Was God.** By ordinary historical standards, the best
  explanation is that **Jesus claimed divine authority.** Answers the most common objection ("but he
  never says 'I am God'") with the **implicit-claim analogy** ("*my wife and I just celebrated our
  anniversary*" claims marriage without the word) and by noting Jesus' authority surfaces all through
  Mark — **over sacred things, nature, demons, disease, and death.** Shows **continuity** between
  Jesus' self-claims and the church's confession; closes on sharing this **graciously** (1 Peter 3:15).
  The book's own conclusion quotes the **Nicene Creed** + the **1 Cor 15:3–5 creed** (Dunn "within
  months"). *Cross-map: `ev-m-jesus_claims`, `library/earlycreed.html`.*

## ⚠️ Calibration flags (carry into any use)
1. **"Best explanation / very probable," NOT proof** — the whole book is inference-to-best-explanation
   on agreed historical data; never inflate to "history proves Jesus is God." Matches our guardrail.
2. **Divine *authority*, argued from the *enemies'* reaction** — this is the book's strength (hostile
   testimony / embarrassment). Keep the enemies-reaction framing; it's more robust than "his followers
   said so."
3. **"Share God's throne" → divine-identity inclusion, not a lesser/second deity** (ch. 8/9) — fence
   with an Is-saying/Not-saying `orthonote` on our pages.
4. **Ehrman = literary concession, not historical** (ch. 9) — keep precise.
5. **1 Enoch Similitudes dating is contested** (ch. 9) — use for the *concept*, don't over-date.
6. **Mark-not-John is a *strategic* choice** (meeting skeptics on their ground), NOT a concession that
   John is unhistorical — say so if the point comes up.
