## Usage rules (same as every note in this folder)
- **Never reproduce the book's prose.** Everything here is our paraphrase of the argument's *shape*.
- **Nothing here is quotable until verified.** Every citation below is a **lead**, unverified until
  confirmed against the **primary source** and run through `apologia-citations` → `apologia-argument`
  → `apologia-orthodoxy`. Copyrighted text never enters `/sources`.
- Watch for **popular-level overstatement** (this is a lay-apologetics book) and hold it to the
  argument-specific rules in `CLAUDE.md` — especially the Resurrection rule (lead with the
  1 Cor 15:3–7 creed dated to within ~2–5 years; never "merely written decades later").

## Why this book matters to us
It is the standard popular presentation of **Habermas's "minimal facts" method** — the approach our
Resurrection tab (`ev-s2`) and the `minimalfacts` / `emptytomb` / `paulconv` essays already lean on —
and it is our own **"Further study"** recommendation on several `/answers/*` resurrection pages. A
verified map lets us cite the underlying primaries (creed, scholars, skeptical concessions) directly
and in our own voice, rather than the book.

## Spine / thesis
The resurrection is the central, load-bearing claim of Christianity (Paul: if it didn't happen, the
faith is worthless), and it can be defended **historically** — from a small set of facts that even
critical, non-Christian scholarship grants — rather than by first assuming the Bible is inspired.
The book's aim is explicitly **equipping the ordinary believer to give a reasoned defense**
(1 Peter 3:15 — the same verse that governs this site).

## Chapter map (captured so far)

### Introduction to Part 1 — "Let the Discussion Begin" (pp. 23–24)
- **Beat:** the popular media picture of Jesus is skewed toward the **Jesus Seminar**, a *self-selected*
  fringe group mistaken by the public for mainstream scholarship. Sets up the book's method: argue from
  facts the broad scholarly field grants, not from the Seminar's radical conclusions.
- **Primary-source leads to verify (do NOT quote from the book — chase these to the source):**
  - **Peter Jennings / ABC News, *The Search for Jesus*** (television documentary, aired **July 2000**);
    the book's claim: of ~7 NT scholars interviewed, 4 were Jesus Seminar members, and Seminar
    co-founder **John Dominic Crossan** got ~¼ of the airtime. → verify the doc, air date, and the
    airtime characterization before any use.
  - **Luke Timothy Johnson** (Emory University) — that the Jesus Seminar is **"self-selected,"** not a
    genuine cross-section of the field. Likely from *The Real Jesus* (HarperSanFrancisco, 1996) →
    verify exact wording + page.
  - **Craig L. Blomberg** (Denver Seminary) — that the Seminar reflects only the field's
    **"radical fringe,"** with flawed methodology and unnecessarily skeptical conclusions → verify
    source (essay/interview?) + wording.
  - **John Dominic Crossan** — his own admission that his views do **not** represent most scholars →
    find the primary (Crossan's own writing/interview), not the book's paraphrase.
- **Guardrail fit:** the "Jesus Seminar ≠ consensus" point is legitimate and useful, but keep it
  **charitable and accurate** — it's a claim about *representativeness*, not a licence to caricature
  critical scholarship. Steelman the other side.

### Chapter 1 — "Unwrapping the Gift: Evangelism and the Resurrection of Jesus" (pp. 25–26+)
- **Beat:** defines the **gospel** (from Acts and Paul) as centring on a minimum of **three essential
  facts — (1) the deity of Jesus, (2) his substitutionary death, (3) his resurrection** — then argues
  the resurrection is the *crucial, load-bearing* issue: without it the whole message collapses.
- **Primary-source leads (Scripture — verify wording/reference in a standard translation before use):**
  - **Matthew 28:19–20** — the Great Commission.
  - **Romans 10:8–9** — confess "Jesus is Lord" + believe God "raised him from the dead."
  - **1 Peter 1:3–4** — a living hope / indestructible inheritance "through the resurrection of Jesus
    Christ from the dead."
  - **1 Corinthians 15:14–19** — "if Christ has not been raised… your faith is worthless… you are
    still in your sins"; those who died in Christ are lost. *(This is our tab's anchor — but note our
    rule: lead the historical case with the **1 Cor 15:3–7 creed**, ~2–5 years, which this chapter's
    framing sets up.)*
  - **1 Corinthians 15:32** — "let us eat and drink, for tomorrow we die."
  - **1 Peter 3:15** — "make a defense… with gentleness and reverence" (the book's stated objective;
    our site's governing verse).
- **Guardrail fit:** the "three essential facts" gospel definition is squarely orthodox (deity +
  atoning death + resurrection). When we use the 1 Cor 15 material, apply the **Resurrection rule**:
  lead with the early **creed** (vv. 3–7) and its dating, not "written decades later."
