Arguments for God's Existence

You don't need a philosophy degree to understand why millions of thoughtful people believe God exists. These arguments — some ancient, some built on modern science — give you real reasons to believe, not just feelings. Start with the Kalam if you're new. Go as deep as you like.

Cosmological arguments

01
The Kalam Cosmological Argument
Everything that begins to exist has a cause — including the universe
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: Everything that starts to exist was caused by something. The universe started to exist. So the universe was caused by something — and that something had to exist outside space, time, and matter. That sounds a lot like God.
The three steps
  1. Everything that begins to exist has a cause — you can't get something from nothing.
  2. The universe began to exist — modern science confirms it had a starting point.
  3. Therefore the universe has a cause that exists outside itself — outside space, time, and matter.

Think of it this way. Imagine you walked into a room and found a ball sitting on a table. You'd naturally ask — where did it come from? Now imagine the entire universe is that ball. The universe had a beginning — scientists call it the Big Bang — which means something caused it. But that cause had to exist before space, time, matter, and energy existed. The only thing that fits that description is something timeless, spaceless, and incredibly powerful. Christians say that's God. Philosopher William Lane Craig has made this one of the most discussed arguments in modern philosophy.

Questions people ask
Q But who made God? — The argument only applies to things that begin to exist. God, by definition, never started to exist — he's eternal. So he doesn't need a cause.
Q What about quantum physics? — Quantum particles appear from a quantum vacuum, which already exists and follows physical laws. That's not nothing — that's something. The argument is about absolute nothing.

So the universe was caused by something outside itself — something that existed before space, time and matter. The BGV theorem

The Borde-Guth-Vilenkin (BGV) theorem, developed in 2003 by three secular cosmologists, proves that any universe that has been on average expanding throughout its history cannot be past-eternal — it must have had an absolute beginning. Crucially, Alexander Vilenkin — not a theist — has stated plainly: "All the evidence we have says that the universe had a beginning." This makes the second premise of the Kalam extremely well supported by modern cosmology.

What the cause must be like

The cause of the universe must exist outside space, time, matter and energy — since it caused all of these. It must therefore be: spaceless, timeless, immaterial, enormously powerful, and — since it brought a temporal universe into existence from a timeless state — personal. These properties correspond precisely to the classical conception of God in theistic philosophy.

02
The Fine-Tuning Argument
The physical constants of the universe are calibrated to extraordinary precision for life
📚 Intermediate
Free
In plain English: The universe runs on precise settings — like dials on a control panel. If any of those dials were even slightly off, no stars, no planets, no life would exist. The precision is so exact that many scientists say it looks designed. Christians say it was.
The three steps
  1. The universe has incredibly precise physical settings — if any were even slightly different, life would be impossible.
  2. This extreme precision is not the result of chance or necessity.
  3. The best explanation is that the universe was intentionally designed for life.

Imagine finding a control panel with 20 dials, each of which needs to be set to exactly the right position for the room to light up. You walk in and every single dial is perfect. Would you think that happened by accident? The universe has physical constants — like the strength of gravity and how strongly atoms bond together — that work exactly like those dials. Change any of them even slightly, and the universe collapses, or flies apart, or never forms stars or planets. Physicist Freeman Dyson put it this way: "The universe somehow knew we were coming."

Questions people ask
Q What about the multiverse? — If there are infinite universes, one of them will be fine-tuned by chance. But this requires an enormous, untestable assumption. A designer is a simpler explanation. And even if a multiverse exists, something had to generate it — fine-tuning doesn't disappear.
Pro — Deep Dive The numbers, Robin Collins's work, and the multiverse objection in full

The numbers

Physicist Robin Collins has done the most rigorous work on fine-tuning. The cosmological constant — the energy density of empty space — is fine-tuned to 1 part in 10 to the power of 120. The ratio of electromagnetic force to gravity is fine-tuned to 1 part in 10 to the power of 40. These are not margins of error — they are the difference between a universe that can sustain life and one that cannot. The probability of these values occurring by chance is, by any reasonable measure, functionally zero.

Why the multiverse doesn't solve it

The multiverse hypothesis requires: (1) the existence of a universe-generating mechanism, (2) that this mechanism is capable of producing the range of physical constants we observe, and (3) that it actually produces infinitely many universes. None of these are established. The multiverse is not a scientific theory — it is a philosophical response to fine-tuning. It also generates the "Boltzmann Brain" problem and fails to explain the fine-tuning of the multiverse-generating mechanism itself.

03
The Moral Argument
Objective moral values require a transcendent foundation
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: We all know some things are genuinely wrong — not just unpopular, but actually wrong. Torturing children. Genocide. Slavery. But if there's no God, moral rules are just things humans invented. If morality is real and objective, it needs a foundation. God is that foundation.
The three steps
  1. Objective moral values — things that are genuinely right or wrong regardless of what anyone thinks — exist.
  2. Without God, there's no grounding for objective morality — it would just be personal or cultural preference.
  3. Therefore God exists as the foundation of real, objective moral truth.

Most people feel that some things are genuinely wrong. Not just "I don't like it" wrong, but actually, truly wrong. The Holocaust was evil, not just unfashionable. But if there's no God, those feelings are just evolution and culture. There's no real rule book — just preferences that helped our ancestors survive. Note: this argument is not saying atheists can't be good people. Of course they can. The argument is about what makes something objectively right or wrong, not who behaves well.

Questions people ask
Q Atheists can be moral without God. — Correct — this argument is not about behaviour. It's about what makes an action objectively right or wrong. Even if atheists behave morally, the question is what grounds the objectivity of moral facts.
Pro — Deep Dive The grounding problem, evolutionary debunking, and William Lane Craig

The grounding problem

The core question is not whether moral claims are true, but what makes them true. On atheism, moral facts would need to be grounded in something — human preferences, evolutionary pressures, social contracts — none of which are objective. On theism, moral facts are grounded in the nature of a perfectly good God. This doesn't make morality arbitrary (the Euthyphro dilemma) because God's nature itself is the standard of goodness — he doesn't follow a moral law, he is the moral law.

The Argument from Consciousness
The existence of mind cannot be explained by matter alone
🎓 Advanced
Free
In plain English: Science can describe what happens in your brain when you taste coffee — electrical signals, chemical reactions. But it can't explain why there's something it feels like to taste coffee. That inner experience is almost impossible to explain if the universe is just physical stuff. But if God is a conscious mind who made the universe, minds and experiences make perfect sense.
The three steps
  1. You have inner experiences — the feeling of warmth, the colour red, the taste of something sweet. These are real.
  2. No purely physical explanation can explain why physical processes give rise to inner experience.
  3. A universe created by a conscious God is exactly what you'd expect to produce conscious beings.

Philosopher David Chalmers calls this the "hard problem of consciousness." Even a complete neuroscientific account of all brain processes would leave unexplained why those processes are accompanied by inner experience. If the universe is just matter and energy with no mind behind it, consciousness is deeply strange. But if the universe was created by a personal, conscious God — then the existence of minds makes complete sense.

Pro — Deep Dive The hard problem, qualia, and the theistic explanation

Qualia and the explanatory gap

Qualia are the subjective, felt qualities of experience — the redness of red, the painfulness of pain. Frank Jackson's "Mary's Room" thought experiment illustrates this: Mary knows all physical facts about colour vision but learns something new when she first sees red. That something new — a quale — is not captured by any physical description. This explanatory gap between physical processes and subjective experience is genuine and has not been bridged by neuroscience despite decades of effort.

01
The Minimal Facts Argument
Five facts accepted by virtually all historians — Gary Habermas
🌱 Beginner
The five minimal facts
  1. Jesus died by crucifixion.
  2. His disciples believed he rose and appeared to them.
  3. Paul, a persecutor of Christians, was suddenly converted.
  4. James, a sceptic and Jesus's brother, was suddenly converted.
  5. The tomb was empty.

In plain English: You don't need to trust the Bible to investigate the resurrection. There are five historical facts that even atheist historians accept. Historian Gary Habermas asks: what's the best explanation for all five? He argues the resurrection fits better than any other theory — and he's spent 40 years testing every alternative.

Gary Habermas has a brilliant approach to the resurrection. Instead of saying "trust the Bible," he only uses facts that virtually all serious historians — Christian, atheist, and agnostic — agree on. Then he asks: what theory best explains them all? The stolen body theory? It can't explain why Paul and James — who had every reason to stay hostile to Christianity — completely changed their lives. The hallucination theory? It can't explain the empty tomb. The legend theory? It developed too fast — the creed in 1 Corinthians 15 was being recited when eyewitnesses were still alive. The resurrection is the one explanation that accounts for everything. That's not blind faith — it's following the evidence where it leads.

Q The disciples stole the body. — This doesn't explain Paul's conversion — he was actively persecuting Christians. It also doesn't explain why the disciples would willingly die for what they knew was a lie.

02
The Empty Tomb — Historical Evidence
Multiple independent sources confirm the tomb was found empty
📚 Intermediate
Free

In plain English: Three days after Jesus was buried, his tomb was found empty. The most powerful evidence? His enemies — who wanted to shut down Christianity — never denied the tomb was empty. Instead, they said the disciples stole the body. But if you're explaining why the tomb is empty, you're already admitting it was empty.

The key points
  1. Jesus was buried in a known tomb — provided by a named man, Joseph of Arimathea.
  2. Three days later, multiple people found it empty.
  3. The Jewish leaders — who wanted to stop the resurrection story — accused the disciples of stealing the body instead of saying "no, the body is still there."
  4. That admission that the tomb was empty is some of the strongest evidence we have.

If Jesus's body was still in the tomb, the Jewish authorities had the perfect weapon to stop Christianity instantly. They could have walked to the tomb, pointed to the body, and ended the movement on day one. They didn't do that. Instead, the account in Matthew's Gospel records that they bribed soldiers to say the disciples had stolen the body. Think about what that means. When someone offers an alternative explanation for why something happened, they're already conceding the thing happened. The Jewish leaders conceded the tomb was empty — they just offered a different explanation. Christians say the real explanation is that Jesus rose from the dead.

QThe disciples stole the body. — This is the oldest counter-explanation, recorded in Matthew 28:11-15. It requires the disciples to have died for what they knew was a lie. People die for beliefs they think are true — they don't die for what they know to be fabricated.

The multiple attestation of the empty tomb

Enemy attestation — the most powerful evidence

The most compelling evidence for the empty tomb comes from the enemies of Christianity. Matthew 28:11-15 records that the Jewish chief priests bribed the soldiers to say the disciples stole the body. This counter-story is itself evidence for the empty tomb — it presupposes that the tomb was empty and offers an alternative explanation for why. The Jewish authorities never claimed the body was still in the tomb. They never produced the body. Instead they were forced to explain the empty tomb. This is enemy attestation of the highest order.

Women as the primary witnesses

All four Gospels record women as the primary witnesses to the empty tomb. In first-century Jewish culture, women's testimony was not accepted in legal proceedings. If the early Christians were fabricating the resurrection story, they would never have chosen women as the primary witnesses — it would undermine the credibility of the account in their cultural context. The fact that women are consistently named as the first witnesses strongly suggests the Gospel writers were recording what actually happened rather than inventing a story designed to persuade.

Alternative theories examined

Wrong tomb theory: Requires that Joseph of Arimathea — who provided the tomb — forgot where it was, that the disciples forgot, that the Jewish authorities forgot, and that no one could find the correct tomb in a small city within days of the burial. Historically implausible.

Swoon theory: Jesus survived the crucifixion, revived in the tomb, pushed away the stone, overpowered the guards, and appeared to his disciples in sufficient health to convince them he had conquered death. Roman soldiers were highly skilled at executing criminals — they verified death before releasing bodies. The spear thrust into Jesus's side (John 19:34) and the water and blood that flowed out indicate death had already occurred.

Hallucination theory: The disciples hallucinated the appearances but the tomb was actually occupied. This fails because the Jewish authorities could simply have produced the body. It also requires mass hallucinations — which are not documented in psychological literature.

1 Corinthians 15 preserves a creed dating within years of the crucifixion

The key points
  1. Paul actively persecuted and killed Christians before his conversion — he had every reason to stay opposed to Christianity.
  2. He claims a dramatic encounter with the risen Jesus completely changed him.
  3. He then quotes a list of resurrection witnesses in 1 Corinthians 15 — a tradition scholars date to within 2-5 years of the crucifixion.

Saul of Tarsus was the ancient world's equivalent of an anti-Christian extremist. He stood by approving as Stephen was stoned to death. He dragged Christians from their homes. Paul includes an appearance to James specifically in the 1 Corinthians 15 creed. The conversion of a family member who was sceptical of Jesus's ministry is one of the most psychologically compelling pieces of evidence for the resurrection. People don't die for a lie about their brother whom they watched grow up.

The Historical Jesus

01
The Historical Existence of Jesus — Non-Christian Sources
Secular historians confirm the existence of Jesus independently of the New Testament
🌱 Beginner
Free

In plain English: Some people claim Jesus never existed at all. But this view is rejected by virtually every serious historian — including atheists. Roman and Jewish writers who had no reason to support Christianity still mentioned Jesus. The evidence is actually stronger than for many famous figures of ancient history.

The key sources
  1. Tacitus — a Roman historian who disliked Christians — confirmed Jesus was executed under Pontius Pilate.
  2. Josephus — a Jewish historian — referred to Jesus and his brother James in his historical records.
  3. Pliny the Younger — a Roman governor — described Christians worshipping Jesus as God within 80 years of the crucifixion.
  4. Even Jesus's enemies never denied he existed — they disputed his claims, not his existence.

Here's the thing — we don't have YouTube footage of Julius Caesar either. We accept ancient historical figures based on documents and records from their time. By that same standard, the evidence for Jesus is actually very strong. The Roman historian Tacitus — who had zero sympathy for Christianity, calling it a "destructive superstition" — still confirmed that Jesus was executed by Pontius Pilate during the reign of Tiberius. That's exactly what the Gospels say. Even Bart Ehrman, an agnostic New Testament scholar who has written books arguing against traditional Christianity, wrote an entire book defending the historical existence of Jesus — because the evidence is that clear. The people who say Jesus never existed are not following the evidence. They're rejecting it.

Tacitus — Annals 15.44 (c. 116 AD)

The Roman historian Tacitus, writing around 116 AD, describes Nero's persecution of Christians following the great fire of Rome in 64 AD. He writes that the Christians derived their name from "Christus, who was executed at the hands of the procurator Pontius Pilate in the reign of Tiberius." This passage is considered authentic by virtually all scholars — Tacitus was not sympathetic to Christianity, describing it as a "destructive superstition," making fabrication or interpolation extremely unlikely. It independently confirms the crucifixion under Pilate, exactly as the Gospels record.

Josephus — Antiquities of the Jews (c. 93 AD)

The Jewish historian Josephus refers to Jesus twice in his Antiquities. The shorter reference — describing the stoning of "James the brother of Jesus called Christ" — is accepted as authentic by the vast majority of scholars across the ideological spectrum. The longer Testimonium Flavianum passage is more complex: most scholars believe it contains authentic Josephan material that was later embellished by Christian scribes. The Arabic version of Josephus, discovered in the 10th century, preserves what appears to be a less-embellished form of the passage, still referring to Jesus as a wise man who performed surprising deeds.

Pliny the Younger — Letter to Trajan (c. 112 AD)

The Roman governor Pliny the Younger wrote to Emperor Trajan around 112 AD describing his investigation of Christians in Bithynia. He records that Christians "sing hymns to Christ as to a god" and meet on a fixed day before dawn. While this doesn't confirm biographical details about Jesus, it confirms that within 80 years of the crucifixion, Christians in Asia Minor were worshipping him as divine — far too early for legend to have entirely displaced historical memory.

Mara bar Serapion — Letter from prison (c. late 1st century)

A Syrian philosopher writing from prison to his son refers to "the wise king of the Jews" who was executed but whose teachings live on — most scholars identify this as a reference to Jesus. This represents an independent non-Roman, non-Jewish source confirming the broad outlines of Jesus's execution and the survival of his movement.

The criterion of embarrassment and enemy attestation

Two important historical criteria further confirm the historical Jesus. The criterion of embarrassment notes that early Christian sources contain details that would have been embarrassing if invented — Jesus's baptism by John (implying subordination), his crucifixion as a criminal (a stumbling block to Jews), his Galilean origins, and the flight of his disciples. No one inventing a religious founder would include these details. Enemy attestation notes that even Jesus's enemies — Jewish leaders and Roman authorities — never denied he existed; they disputed his claims, not his existence. The Talmud contains references to Jesus that are hostile but presuppose his historical reality.

The Criterion of Multiple Attestation
Events confirmed by multiple independent sources are more likely to be historical
📚 Intermediate
Free
The argument in brief
  1. Historians grant greater credibility to events attested by multiple independent sources.
  2. Many key facts about Jesus — his baptism, his crucifixion, his reputation as a healer, his resurrection appearances — are attested by multiple independent sources.
  3. Multiple independent attestation therefore provides strong historical grounds for accepting these facts about Jesus.

In plain English: Historians trust events more when multiple independent sources all mention them. The crucifixion of Jesus is mentioned by four separate Gospel writers, Paul, Tacitus, and Josephus — people who didn't copy from each other. The more independent sources confirm something, the more confident historians can be it really happened.

If you hear a rumour from one person, you might be sceptical. If you hear the same thing from five people who don't know each other and had no chance to coordinate, you start to believe it. Historians use the same instinct — they call it multiple attestation. A historical event attested in multiple independent sources is much more likely to be real. The crucifixion of Jesus is attested in Mark's Gospel, Paul's letters, Tacitus, and Josephus — sources that are independent of each other. The resurrection appearances are attested in multiple Gospel traditions and Paul's early creed. This gives historians good reasons to take these events seriously as history, not just religious story.

Pro — Deep Dive
Key facts about Jesus multiply attested across independent sources

What counts as independent attestation

For sources to provide independent attestation, they must not have copied from each other. In New Testament studies, scholars generally identify multiple independent source traditions: Mark (the earliest Gospel), Q (the hypothetical source shared by Matthew and Luke but not from Mark), the material unique to Matthew (M), the material unique to Luke (L), John (largely independent of the Synoptics), and Paul's letters (which predate the Gospels). When an event or saying appears in multiple of these independent traditions, it has multiple attestation. Additionally, non-Christian sources (Tacitus, Josephus, Pliny) provide independent external attestation for basic facts.

Key multiply attested facts about Jesus

His baptism by John: Attested in Mark, Q, and John — and is an embarrassing detail (implying subordination to John) that no one would invent. His crucifixion under Pontius Pilate: Attested in Mark, Paul, John, Tacitus, and Josephus — one of the most historically secure facts about Jesus. His reputation as a healer and exorcist: Attested in multiple Gospel traditions and acknowledged even by his enemies who attributed his miracles to demonic power (a concession that implicitly admits the miracles occurred). His resurrection appearances: Attested in Paul's early creed (1 Cor 15), Mark's empty tomb tradition, and independent appearance traditions in Luke, John, and Matthew.

Applying consistent standards

The criterion of multiple attestation is not unique to Christian apologetics — it is standard historiography. Julius Caesar's campaigns are attested by Caesar himself, Cicero, Plutarch, and others. Alexander the Great's conquests are attested by Arrian, Plutarch, and Diodorus. The New Testament provides at minimum four independent source traditions for Jesus — more independent attestation than most figures of antiquity. Applying consistent historical standards requires treating this evidence with the same seriousness applied to other ancient figures.

The immediate reaction of his audience — picking up stones to execute him for blasphemy — confirms they understood exactly what he was claiming.

John's Gospel was providing accurate topographical information about Jerusalem.

In 1990, a burial cave south of Jerusalem was accidentally discovered during construction work. The RNA World and its problems

The most widely discussed naturalistic hypothesis for the origin of life is the RNA World — the idea that RNA molecules, which can both store information and catalyse chemical reactions, preceded DNA and protein-based life. Second, even short functional RNA sequences require specific arrangements of nucleotides — the probability of generating them by chance is astronomically small. Third, RNA is chemically fragile and degrades rapidly in water — the environment in which it supposedly arose. After decades of research, the RNA World remains a hypothesis without a demonstrated pathway.

Both involve sequences of symbols that are: (1) physically arbitrary (any sequence is chemically possible), (2) functionally specified (only certain sequences work), and (3) capable of being expressed as instructions that direct physical processes. In all of human experience, such information has one and only one known cause — intelligence. This is not an argument from ignorance but from our best understanding of how information arises. As information theorist Hubert Yockey concluded after a lifetime of study: "The origin of the genetic code is the most baffling aspect of the problem of the origin of life and a major conceptual or philosophical obstacle."

Crick's honest acknowledgement of the difficulty, even while rejecting theistic explanation, is a powerful indicator of how intractable the problem genuinely is.
