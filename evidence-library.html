Arguments for God's Existence

You don't need a philosophy degree to understand why millions of thoughtful people believe God exists. Start with the Kalam if you're new. Go as deep as you like.

Cosmological arguments

01
The Kalam Cosmological Argument
Everything that begins to exist has a cause — including the universe
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: Everything that starts to exist was caused by something. The universe started to exist. So the universe was caused by something outside itself — outside space, time, and matter. That sounds a lot like God.
The three steps
  1. Everything that begins to exist has a cause — you can't get something from nothing.
  2. The universe began to exist — modern science confirms it had a starting point.
  3. Therefore the universe has a cause outside itself — outside space, time, and matter.

The universe had a beginning — scientists call it the Big Bang. That cause had to exist before space, time, matter, and energy existed. The only thing that fits is something timeless, spaceless, and incredibly powerful. Philosopher William Lane Craig has made this one of the most discussed arguments in modern philosophy.

Questions people ask
QWho made God? — The argument only applies to things that begin to exist. God never started to exist — he's eternal. He doesn't need a cause.
QWhat about quantum physics? — Quantum particles appear from a quantum vacuum, which already exists. That's not nothing. The argument is about absolute nothing.
Pro — Deep DiveFull analysis, BGV theorem, and extended objections

The BGV theorem

The Borde-Guth-Vilenkin theorem proves that any universe that has been on average expanding cannot be past-eternal — it must have had a beginning. Vilenkin — not a theist — stated: "All the evidence we have says the universe had a beginning."

What the cause must be like

The cause must be spaceless, timeless, immaterial, and enormously powerful. Since it brought a temporal universe into existence from a timeless state, it must be personal. These properties correspond precisely to the classical conception of God.

02
The Fine-Tuning Argument
The physical constants of the universe are calibrated for life
📚 Intermediate
Free
In plain English: The universe runs on precise settings — like dials on a control panel. If any dial were even slightly off, no stars, no planets, no life would exist. Many scientists say it looks designed. Christians say it was.
The three steps
  1. The universe has incredibly precise physical settings — change any and life becomes impossible.
  2. This extreme precision is not the result of chance or necessity.
  3. The best explanation is intentional design.

The cosmological constant is fine-tuned to 1 part in 10 to the power of 120. If it were slightly different, the universe would either collapse or fly apart. Physicist Freeman Dyson: "The universe somehow knew we were coming."

Questions people ask
QWhat about the multiverse? — This requires enormous untestable assumptions. And even a multiverse-generating mechanism would itself need explanation. Fine-tuning doesn't disappear.
Pro — Deep DiveThe numbers, Robin Collins, and the multiverse objection in full

The numbers

Robin Collins has done the most rigorous work on fine-tuning. The cosmological constant is fine-tuned to 1 in 10^120. The ratio of electromagnetic force to gravity is fine-tuned to 1 in 10^40. These are not margins of error — they are the difference between a life-permitting universe and one that cannot exist.

03
The Moral Argument
Objective moral values require a transcendent foundation
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: We all know some things are genuinely wrong — not just unpopular, but actually wrong. If there's no God, moral rules are just things humans invented. If morality is real and objective, it needs a foundation. God is that foundation.
The three steps
  1. Objective moral values — things genuinely right or wrong regardless of what anyone thinks — exist.
  2. Without God there's no grounding for objective morality — just personal preference.
  3. Therefore God exists as the foundation of real objective moral truth.

The Holocaust was evil — not just unpopular. On a purely atheistic view, those feelings are just evolution and culture. There's no real rule book. But we sense that there is one. Christians say that feeling is correct — because God exists as the standard of perfect goodness.

Questions people ask
QAtheists can be moral without God. — Correct — this argument is not about behaviour. It's about what makes an action objectively right or wrong. Even if atheists behave morally, the question is what grounds the objectivity of moral facts.
Pro — Deep DiveThe grounding problem and William Lane Craig

The grounding problem

The core question is not whether moral claims are true, but what makes them true. On atheism, moral facts would need to be grounded in human preferences or evolutionary pressures — none of which are objective. On theism, moral facts are grounded in the nature of a perfectly good God. God doesn't follow a moral law — he is the moral law.

04
The Argument from Consciousness
The existence of mind cannot be explained by matter alone
🎓 Advanced
Free
In plain English: Science can describe what happens in your brain when you taste coffee. But it can't explain why there's something it feels like to taste coffee. If God is a conscious mind who made the universe, minds and inner experiences make perfect sense.
The three steps
  1. You have inner experiences — the feeling of warmth, the colour red, the taste of something sweet. These are real.
  2. No purely physical explanation can explain why physical processes give rise to inner experience.
  3. A universe created by a conscious God is exactly what you'd expect to produce conscious beings.

Philosopher David Chalmers calls this the "hard problem of consciousness." Even a complete neuroscientific account would leave unexplained why brain processes are accompanied by inner experience. If God is a conscious mind who created the universe, the existence of conscious beings makes complete sense.

Pro — Deep DiveThe hard problem, qualia, and the theistic explanation

Qualia and the explanatory gap

Frank Jackson's "Mary's Room" thought experiment: Mary knows all physical facts about colour vision but learns something new when she first sees red. That something — a quale — is not captured by any physical description. This explanatory gap between physical processes and subjective experience is genuine and has not been bridged by neuroscience.

Category 2 of 5

Evidence for the Resurrection

Christianity stands or falls on one question — did Jesus actually rise from the dead? This isn't just a matter of faith. It's a historical question with real evidence. You don't need to trust the Bible as Scripture to follow these arguments.

Minimal facts approach

01
The Minimal Facts Argument
Five facts accepted by virtually all historians — including atheists
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: You don't need to trust the Bible to investigate the resurrection. There are five historical facts that even atheist historians accept. Historian Gary Habermas asks: what's the best explanation for all five? He argues the resurrection fits better than any other theory.
The five facts
  1. Jesus died by crucifixion — confirmed by Roman and Jewish sources outside the Bible.
  2. His tomb was found empty three days later — even his enemies admitted this.
  3. His disciples genuinely believed they saw him alive after death.
  4. Paul — who used to kill Christians — converted after claiming to see the risen Jesus.
  5. James — Jesus's own brother, a sceptic — converted and later died for his belief.

Gary Habermas only uses facts that virtually all serious historians agree on. Then he asks: what theory best explains them all? Hallucination? Doesn't explain the empty tomb. Stolen body? Doesn't explain Paul and James. Legend? Developed too fast. The resurrection is the one explanation that accounts for everything.

Questions people ask
QThe disciples hallucinated. — Group hallucinations of the same content are not documented in psychology. More importantly, hallucinations don't explain the empty tomb or the conversion of hostile witnesses Paul and James.
QThe disciples stole the body. — This doesn't explain Paul's conversion — he was actively persecuting Christians. People die for beliefs they think are true, not for what they know is a lie.
Pro — Deep DiveAll five facts examined in depth with full scholarly sources

The hostile witnesses

Paul was a Pharisee who actively persecuted and killed Christians. James was Jesus's own brother who thought Jesus was crazy during his ministry (John 7:5). Both converted based on claimed resurrection appearances. The conversion of hostile, sceptical witnesses is one of the most historically compelling pieces of evidence. People don't die for a lie about their brother whom they watched grow up.

02
The Empty Tomb
Multiple independent sources confirm the tomb was found empty
📚 Intermediate
Free
In plain English: Three days after Jesus was buried, his tomb was found empty. The most powerful evidence? His enemies never denied the tomb was empty. Instead they said the disciples stole the body. If you're explaining why the tomb is empty, you're already admitting it was empty.
The key points
  1. Jesus was buried in a known tomb provided by a named man, Joseph of Arimathea.
  2. Three days later multiple people found it empty.
  3. The Jewish leaders accused the disciples of stealing the body — conceding the tomb was empty.
  4. If the body was still there, the authorities could have ended Christianity immediately by producing it.

The Jewish leaders bribed soldiers to say the disciples had stolen the body (Matthew 28). Think about what that means — when someone offers an alternative explanation for why something happened, they're conceding the thing happened. The Jewish leaders conceded the tomb was empty.

Pro — Deep DiveFull historical analysis and all alternative theories examined

Women as primary witnesses

All four Gospels record women as the primary witnesses to the empty tomb. In first-century Jewish culture, women's testimony was not accepted in legal proceedings. If the early Christians were fabricating the story, they would never have chosen women as the primary witnesses — it would undermine credibility. The fact that women are consistently named strongly suggests the writers were recording what actually happened.

03
Paul's Conversion and the Early Creed
1 Corinthians 15 dates to within years of the crucifixion
🎓 Advanced
Free
In plain English: Paul was a man who hunted and killed Christians — then became one of their greatest advocates and died for his faith. He quotes a list of resurrection witnesses that scholars date to within 2-5 years of the crucifixion — when eyewitnesses were still alive to be questioned.
The key points
  1. Paul actively persecuted Christians before his conversion — he had every reason to stay hostile.
  2. He claims a dramatic encounter with the risen Jesus completely changed him.
  3. He quotes a resurrection witnesses list in 1 Corinthians 15 dated to within 2-5 years of the crucifixion.
  4. He says most of those 500+ witnesses were still alive and could be questioned.

Most scholars date the 1 Corinthians 15 creed to within 2-5 years of the crucifixion. The legend theory — that the resurrection story grew slowly like a game of telephone — cannot survive that timeline. This creed was circulating when eyewitnesses were still alive and accessible.

Pro — Deep DiveThe creed's early dating, Paul's conversion, and James the sceptic

Dating the creed

Paul wrote 1 Corinthians approximately 55 AD. But the creed predates the letter — Paul "received" it. In Galatians 1:18, Paul says he visited Peter three years after his conversion. His conversion is dated to 33-35 AD. This means Paul received this creed within 3-7 years of the crucifixion — confirmed by eyewitnesses Peter and James. New Testament scholar James Dunn calls this "historical bedrock."

Category 3 of 5

The person of Jesus Christ

Who exactly was Jesus? Did he really exist? Did the Old Testament predict him? Did he actually claim to be God? You might be surprised how strong the historical evidence is — even from sources that had no reason to support Christianity.

The Historical Jesus

01
The Historical Existence of Jesus
Secular historians confirm the existence of Jesus independently of the New Testament
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: Some people claim Jesus never existed. But this view is rejected by virtually every serious historian — including atheists. Roman and Jewish writers who had no reason to support Christianity still mentioned Jesus. The evidence is stronger than for many famous figures of ancient history.
The key sources
  1. Tacitus — a Roman historian who disliked Christians — confirmed Jesus was executed under Pontius Pilate.
  2. Josephus — a Jewish historian — referred to Jesus and his brother James.
  3. Pliny the Younger — a Roman governor — described Christians worshipping Jesus as God within 80 years of the crucifixion.
  4. Even Jesus's enemies never denied he existed — they disputed his claims, not his existence.

Even Bart Ehrman — an agnostic New Testament scholar who has written books arguing against traditional Christianity — wrote an entire book defending the historical existence of Jesus. "Whatever else you might think about Jesus, he certainly existed. The evidence is overwhelming."

Pro — Deep DiveFull analysis of every non-Christian source

Tacitus — Annals 15.44

The Roman historian Tacitus, writing around 116 AD, confirms that Christians derived their name from "Christus, who was executed at the hands of the procurator Pontius Pilate in the reign of Tiberius." Tacitus was not sympathetic to Christianity — he called it a "destructive superstition" — making fabrication extremely unlikely. This independently confirms the crucifixion under Pilate, exactly as the Gospels record.

02
Did Jesus Claim to Be God?
Jesus made divine claims his Jewish audience understood clearly
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: People often say "Jesus was a great teacher but I don't think he was God." C.S. Lewis said that's the one option you can't take. Jesus claimed to forgive sins, to be the judge of all humanity, and to have existed before Abraham. His audience tried to stone him for blasphemy. A mere teacher doesn't get stoned for his teaching tips.
The three options — C.S. Lewis's trilemma
  1. Lord — Jesus is who he claimed to be. His claims were true.
  2. Liar — Jesus knew his claims were false but made them anyway.
  3. Lunatic — Jesus genuinely believed he was God but was tragically mistaken.

In John 8:58 Jesus said "Before Abraham was born, I AM" — using the exact divine name from Exodus 3:14. His audience immediately picked up stones to execute him for blasphemy. In Matthew 25 he described himself as the final judge of all humanity — a role that belongs to God alone in Jewish thought. C.S. Lewis: "Good teacher is not on the table."

Pro — Deep DiveEvery major divine claim examined with scholarly analysis

The I AM claims

The Greek phrase ego eimi (I AM) appears seven times in John echoing Exodus 3:14 — where God revealed his name to Moses. Jesus says "I AM the bread of life," "I AM the light of the world," "I AM the resurrection and the life." But the most explicit is John 8:58 — "Before Abraham was born, I AM" — applying the divine name in an absolute, non-predicate form. His audience's immediate attempt to stone him confirms they understood exactly what he was claiming.

The reliability of the Bible

Has the Bible been changed? Is it historically accurate? The evidence from ancient manuscripts, archaeology, and historical records gives us strong grounds to trust what we're reading.

Manuscript evidence

01
The Manuscript Reliability Argument
The New Testament is the most well-attested document in ancient history
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: People say the Bible has been changed over the years. But we can check. We have over 5,800 ancient copies of the New Testament — far more than any other ancient book. When scholars compare them, the text is remarkably consistent. The Bible you read today is extremely close to what was originally written.
The key facts
  1. Over 5,800 Greek New Testament manuscripts — more than any other ancient document by far.
  2. Earliest fragments date within 30-40 years of the original writing.
  3. Scholars can reconstruct the original text with over 99% confidence.
  4. No other ancient text comes remotely close to this level of attestation.

Julius Caesar's Gallic Wars: 10 manuscripts, earliest copy 900 years after Caesar died. The New Testament: 5,800+ manuscripts, earliest fragments within a generation of the events. When scholars compare all those manuscripts, over 99% of the text is not in any serious doubt. The variations are mostly spelling differences.

Pro — Deep DiveManuscript counts, early dating, and what textual variants actually mean

What textual variants actually are

Critics point to "thousands of variants" between manuscripts. This sounds alarming until you understand what a variant is. The vast majority are spelling differences, word order variations, and minor scribal errors. A significant number affect meaning in some way, but virtually none affect any core Christian doctrine. The abundance of manuscripts is actually what allows scholars to detect and correct these variations — more manuscripts means more confidence, not less.

02
Archaeological Confirmation of the Bible
Hundreds of archaeological discoveries confirm biblical accounts
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: Time and again, critics have said "that person or place in the Bible is made up" — and then archaeologists dig it up. The Hittites. Pontius Pilate. The Pool of Bethesda. Each was called a legend, then confirmed by discovery.
Key discoveries
  1. The Hittites — once called legendary — confirmed by a major excavation in 1906.
  2. Pontius Pilate — confirmed by a stone inscription found in 1961.
  3. The Pool of Bethesda — found exactly where John said it was, with the five colonnades he described.
  4. The ossuary of Caiaphas — the high priest who tried Jesus — found in 1990.

Archaeologist William Albright: "Discovery after discovery has established the accuracy of innumerable details, and has brought increased recognition of the Bible as a source of history." Writers who get the verifiable details right deserve to be taken seriously on the larger claims.

Pro — Deep DiveKey archaeological discoveries examined in detail

The Pilate Stone

In 1961, Italian archaeologists excavating Caesarea Maritima discovered a limestone block inscribed: "[Pon]tius Pilatus, [Praef]ectus Iuda[eae]" — Pontius Pilate, Prefect of Judaea. This is the only extant contemporary inscription bearing Pilate's name. The Gospels accurately identified his title (prefect, not procurator as later sources erroneously called him) and his role. A figure once questioned as legendary is now confirmed in stone.

Category 5 of 5

Science & Christian Faith

Science and faith are not enemies. Some of the strongest evidence for God comes from science itself — the origin of the universe, fine-tuning of physical constants, and the complexity of DNA all point toward a Creator.

Origins & cosmology

01
The Argument from the Big Bang
Modern cosmology confirms the universe had an absolute beginning
🌿 Beginner
Free
In plain English: For a long time atheists preferred an eternal universe — if it always existed, you don't need a Creator. Then science proved the universe had a beginning. That's a massive problem for atheism and a huge confirmation of what Genesis 1:1 said all along.
The key facts
  1. The Big Bang model shows the universe began from an initial singularity.
  2. The BGV theorem proves any expanding universe must have had a beginning.
  3. Space, time, matter, and energy all came into existence at this point.
  4. Whatever caused the universe must be outside space, time, and matter.

Cosmologist Alexander Vilenkin — not a theist — stated plainly: "All the evidence we have says the universe had a beginning." Genesis 1:1 said this thousands of years ago. For the universe to begin, something outside the universe had to cause it. Christians have a name for that.

Pro — Deep DiveThe BGV theorem and what caused the Big Bang

The BGV theorem in plain English

Borde, Guth, and Vilenkin proved in 2003 that any universe which has been on average expanding throughout its history cannot be past-eternal. It must have had a beginning. This applies not just to our universe but to any proposed multiverse-generating mechanism. The beginning is inescapable. And a beginning requires a cause that exists outside the universe — outside space, time, matter, and energy. That cause must be personal since it chose to create from a timeless state.

02
The Origin of Life Argument
The information content of DNA points beyond unguided chemistry
📚 Intermediate
Free
In plain English: Evolution explains how life changes once it exists. But it can't explain where the first living cell came from. The first cell needed working DNA — a molecule packed with precise, functional information. In all human experience, information like that comes from one source: a mind.
The key points
  1. Life requires DNA — a molecule storing enormous amounts of precise functional information.
  2. Evolution can't explain the first DNA — you need life to already exist for evolution to work.
  3. Every time we see precisely coded information it comes from a mind.
  4. The origin of the first living cell points toward an intelligent Creator.

Francis Crick — who discovered DNA's double-helix structure and was a committed atheist — said the origin of life "appears almost a miracle." Scientists have been trying to solve this for over 70 years. The more we learn about cellular complexity, the harder the problem becomes. Christians say there's a simpler explanation.

Pro — Deep DiveDNA information, the RNA world problem, and Stephen Meyer

DNA as specified information

Philosopher of science Stephen Meyer argues in Signature in the Cell that DNA contains specified information — not just complexity but functional specification, like a computer program. We encounter specified complexity constantly in human artefacts: computer code, written language, engineered machines. In every case we can examine, specified complexity traces back to intelligence. DNA exhibits exactly this kind of information. The inference to an intelligent cause is not a god-of-the-gaps argument — it's an inference to the best explanation based on what we know about information sources.
