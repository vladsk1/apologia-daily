Faith & Reason · The Call to Defend
"Always be prepared to give an answer to everyone who asks you to give the reason for the hope that you have — but do this with gentleness and respect."
1 Peter 3:15
Peter's instruction to the early church is both a calling and a comfort. The word translated "answer" is the Greek apologia — a legal defence, a reasoned account. Peter is not asking Christians to win arguments. He is asking them to love people enough to think carefully about truth on their behalf.

Notice the conditions Peter adds: gentleness and respect. The best apologists are not the loudest or the most combative. They are the most patient, the most honest, and the most genuinely curious about the person in front of them. They win the hearing before they make the case.

Today, think about someone in your life who has genuine questions about faith. Not a hostile opponent — a genuine seeker. What would it mean to be prepared to meet them? What would it mean to do so with gentleness and respect rather than defensiveness?

Today's prayer

Lord, give me a prepared mind and a gentle spirit. When conversations about faith arise — whether I expect them or not — help me to be present, to listen well, and to speak with both clarity and compassion. Let me never argue to win, but always speak to love. Where I lack knowledge, give me humility. Where I lack courage, give me faith. May the reason for the hope I have be visible not only in what I say, but in how I live. Amen.
