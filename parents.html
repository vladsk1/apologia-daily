👪 For Parents

Raise kids who can keep their faith

Your children will face the hardest questions long before university. This is how to answer them — at every age — with confidence and grace, built on what the research actually says about how kids form belief.

Why earlier than you think

Most parents wait for the teen years. The research says that's too late to lay the foundation. A child's worldview begins forming before age two and is largely set by 13 — and preteens (8–12) are already quietly deciding what they believe. The goal isn't to turn your 5-year-old into a philosopher; it's to weave "is it true, and how do we know?" into normal conversation from the start, changing the method as they grow, not the start date.
