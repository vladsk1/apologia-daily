What is apologetics?

The word "apologetics" comes from the Greek word apologia — a legal defence, a reasoned account. When Peter says "give an answer," he uses this word. He's telling Christians to be prepared to give a thoughtful, reasoned defence of their faith.

Apologetics is not about being argumentative. It's not about making people feel stupid. It's about loving people enough to think carefully about truth on their behalf. The best apologists are not the most aggressive — they're the most patient, the most honest, and the most genuinely curious about the person they're talking to.

Over the next five days, you'll discover that Christianity is not just a feeling or a tradition. It's a faith grounded in evidence — historical, philosophical, and scientific. You'll leave this path with real reasons to believe, and the words to share them naturally in conversation.
